## 1. Finding

GE Renewable Energy OEM Service Manual (filed **VTM-GE-2023**, internal revision tag "2.4") is the currently controlled document for service on the 2.x-series wind turbines deployed at NW-PowerPool-WIND-014, including WIN-10016 and WIN-10058. In June 2023, GE deployed a coordinated firmware refresh (Bulletin GE-SB-2023-014) to the affected fleet that materially changed:

1. Pitch-system control-loop timing (5 ms control loop → 2 ms control loop).
2. Yaw-system fault-tolerance threshold logic (legacy single-fault detection → dual-fault voting).
3. Drivetrain over-temperature trip setpoint (90 °C generator stator → 87 °C, with new pre-trip warning at 82 °C).
4. SoC (state-of-charge) battery management for emergency pitch-shutdown.

The VTM-GE-2023 manual was published in February 2023 — four months before the firmware refresh. Inspection cadences and procedural steps in §4 ("Periodic Inspection — Operating Asset") are calibrated to pre-refresh setpoints and signal timings. GE committed in writing (correspondence dated 2024-01-19) to deliver a Rev. 3 manual reflecting the firmware refresh. As of this CAR issue date, **no Rev. 3 has been received**. The follow-up commitment was for 2024-Q3 delivery; subsequent commitment dates of 2025-Q1, 2025-Q2, and 2025-Q4 have all lapsed.

Field maintenance is therefore being performed against a manual whose inspection procedures cannot reconcile with deployed asset behavior. Two specific operational signatures have emerged.

## 2. Evidence

### 2.1 Procedural-deployed-behavior mismatches

| Manual Procedure | VTM-GE-2023 Specification | Post-Refresh Reality | Impact |
|---|---|---|---|
| §4.2.3 Pitch loop test | Inject step input, observe ≤120 ms response | Deployed loop responds in ≤45 ms; manual procedure flags as out-of-spec (false positive) | Field technicians log unnecessary "anomaly" tickets |
| §4.5.1 Yaw fault simulation | Single fault injection; expect alarm | Dual-fault voting requires simultaneous injection; manual procedure cannot trigger alarm; technicians report "no alarm" as pass | False-negative; actual yaw fault tolerance not verified |
| §4.7.2 Generator over-temperature simulation | Inject 90 °C signal; expect trip | Trip occurs at 87 °C — earlier than expected, manual procedure produces a "premature trip" finding | False anomaly flag |
| §4.8.4 SoC test | Procedure assumes 8.5 V emergency floor | Refresh moved emergency floor to 9.2 V | Test fails on healthy assets |

### 2.2 Linked outages

- OUT-2025-0345 (2025-04-27, WIN-10016, soc_drift, 219 h, 2,506.8 MWh lost) — root cause hypothesized as SoC trip threshold drift; investigation hampered because the §4.8.4 procedure produces false fails on the deployed firmware. Investigation took 38 days to close, of which 12 days were the documentation-misalignment delay.
- OUT-2025-0433 (2025-05-02, WIN-10058, soc_drift, 227 h, 2,506.8 MWh lost) — same fleet, same uncertainty about SoC-test interpretation.
- OUT-2025-0336 (2025-04-01, WIN-10016, overheating, 175 h, 1,932.5 MWh lost) — generator stator over-temperature; manual specifies different setpoint than deployed.
- WIN-10016 has accumulated 16 forced outages in FY2025; WIN-10058 has accumulated 12. Both are on the same site (NW-PowerPool-WIND-014). Both rely on VTM-GE-2023.

### 2.4 Workaround currently in place

Cascadia Engineering issued **Field Bulletin FB-2024-007** ("Interim Inspection Procedure for Refresh-2023 GE Fleet") on 2024-03-15. FB-2024-007 supersedes specific sections of VTM-GE-2023 but has not been integrated into the controlled-documentation system. Field technicians are nominally to apply FB-2024-007 on top of VTM-GE-2023; in practice, audit shows only 64% of inspections cite FB-2024-007 in their work-order documentation.

## 3. Root Cause Hypothesis

- **Primary:** GE has not delivered a firmware-current manual revision. The accountability for delivery rests with GE; the accountability for managing the gap rests with Cascadia OEM Liaison.
- **Contributing:** FB-2024-007 is an unintegrated workaround. It is not a controlled-document object in the engineering documentation system; it exists as a PDF in an Engineering SharePoint. It does not appear in CMMS work-order pre-population.
- **Contributing:** No escalation path was activated when GE missed the 2024-Q3 delivery commitment. The Vendor Performance Review process (cross-reference Procurement Policy PP-2023-04 §9) would have triggered a notice-of-non-conformance at 90 days past commitment.

## 4. Conflict With Existing Guidance

- **VTM-GE-2023** is the controlled document; it conflicts in four specific procedures with the firmware deployed since June 2023.
- **FB-2024-007** is an uncontrolled workaround; its inconsistent application is itself a documentation-control deficiency.
- **Cascadia Engineering Documentation Standard EDS-2022-01 §4.2** requires that all controlled documents reflect deployed asset behavior; this requirement is currently unsatisfiable absent OEM delivery.

Unlike CAR-2025-002 (Siemens Gamesa) and CAR-2025-005 (ABB), this conflict is not between OEM manual and internal standard. It is between OEM manual and physical reality of the OEM's own deployed firmware.

## 5. Recommended Corrective Action

1. Issue formal notice-of-non-conformance to GE under PP-2023-04 §9 within 30 days, citing 18+ months of missed manual-delivery commitments.
2. Reissue FB-2024-007 as a controlled document **CD-FB-2024-007** in the engineering documentation system, with mandatory CMMS pre-population on any GE 2.x-series work order.
3. Suspend §4.2.3, §4.5.1, §4.7.2, and §4.8.4 procedures from VTM-GE-2023 with formal procedure-suspension notice. Replace with the FB-2024-007 procedures pending Rev. 3 delivery.
4. Add a Vendor-Performance contractual remedy for delayed documentation in the next renewal of the GE service relationship.
5. Audit all closed GE work orders 2024-01-01 to date for FB-2024-007 application; re-perform where evidence is missing.
