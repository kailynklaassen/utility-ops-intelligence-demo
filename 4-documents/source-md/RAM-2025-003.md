## 1. Background

On 2025-10-07, WIN-10085 (Vestas wind turbine, NW-PowerPool-WIND-003) tripped on overheating at the generator coolant loop (outage OUT-2025-0476). The forced outage ran 226 hours and produced **3,740.6 MWh of lost generation**. Because CPS was able to mobilize a qualified crew within the Schedule B window (4–8 hours), EPA-2024-11 was **not** invoked. CPS performed the work under the standard MSA.

CPS's report **EMR-2025-004** documents coolant pump degradation, heat exchanger fouling consistent with deferred PM, and a generator that is mechanically within wear tolerance. The vendor's invoice is **$108,000**.

This event is the second overheating event in the Fall Overheating Sequence (refer RCA-2025-002). It is the operational counter-example to the gearbox event at WIN-10131 (EMR-2025-003 / RAM-2025-002) — same period, comparable lost-generation impact, but vendor scope and pricing diverge significantly.

## 2. Vendor Recommendation

CPS (EMR-2025-004, §4) recommends a planned **cooling-system overhaul** at $185K per turbine (CPS Proposal CPS-Q-2025-074). The proposal includes full coolant-loop refurbishment, pump rebuilds, heat-exchanger cleaning, and a coolant-chemistry monitoring upgrade. CPS expressly recommends **no replacement-class capital scope** on the generator, gearbox, or main bearings, citing Cascadia Standard MS-2025-03 §5.2.

## 3. Cascadia Position

Operations concurs with the CPS recommendation. The position is consistent with the refurbishment-over-replacement preference codified in MS-2025-03 and is supported by the observed mechanical condition of the asset. CPS's report explicitly does not invoke "irreversible degradation" or replacement-class language, which Operations notes as a constructive contrast with the pattern observed in Apex EMRs across FY2025 (refer CAR-2025-004).

Vendor Management is filing EMR-2025-004 as a **benchmark document** for the FY2025 Apex performance review. Specifically:

- The CPS emergency scope at WIN-10085 ($108K, 226 hours of outage, 146 labor hours, $185/hr standard rate) costs approximately **25% of equivalent-scope Apex deployments** in FY2025 (e.g., EMR-2025-001 at $412K and EMR-2025-002 at $389K, on similar duration/severity).
- The CPS report makes a refurbishment recommendation; the Apex reports recommend replacement. The pattern is now consistent enough across 4 Apex events to warrant a contract terms review.

NW-PowerPool FY25 maintenance budget impact: this invoice absorbs ~0.8% of the $13.4M annual line. The planned overhaul ($185K) is in the FY26 capital plan request and is not authorized under this memo.

## 5. Approval Request / Decision

**Approved as presented.**

1. The $108K CPS invoice EMR-2025-004 is approved for payment under the standard MSA.
2. The $185K planned cooling-system overhaul (CPS-Q-2025-074) is **noted, not approved here** — it will be processed under the FY26 planned-work authorization track and is cross-referenced to **CAR-2025-002**.
3. Vendor Management files this memo and EMR-2025-004 as a benchmark in **CAR-2025-004** (Apex performance review). Operations requests that Vendor Management complete the Apex contract terms review (rates, surcharges, replacement-bias pattern) by Q1 2026.
4. Operations requests that Procurement evaluate whether CPS Schedule B response can be pre-positioned at NW-PowerPool-WIND-003 and -024 to reduce EPA-2024-11 invocations across the rest of FY26.
