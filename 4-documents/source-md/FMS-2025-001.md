## Section 2 — Significant Events

**1/14, 22:08 PT — WIN-10131 overheating event (first.)**
Gearbox bearing temperature alarm tripped at 22:08 during a cold-weather start cycle. Unit auto-tripped. Operations brought it back online at 04:40 after a 6.5-hour outage. No emergency vendor required — crew was on site for an inspection round. See OIR-2025-005 for the full write-up.

What concerns me is that the temperature trend on WIN-10131 has been creeping for six weeks. The cooling-loop coolant exchange (WO-2025-00146) is the work order that would have caught it. That WO is on the deferred list above.

**1/22 — First Apex dispatch of the year, WIN-10131.**
Repeat alarm on WIN-10131 on 1/21 evening tripped the 4-hour mobilization clock under EPA-2024-11 because CPS could not commit a crew before Friday. Apex mobilized from Spokane, on site by 06:00 on 1/22. Replaced the gearbox oil cooler. Invoice $312,000. That's about $216K more than CPS would have charged for the same scope on a scheduled basis. I am putting it on the record now: we paid premium emergency rates for work we had on the schedule to do for $96K.

**1/26 — Inspection round, WIND-014.**
GE turbines WIN-10058 and WIN-10016 both flagged elevated vibration trend on baseline comparison. Within spec per the deployed Standard MS-2025-03, but the older GE OEM manual (which is what we have to file against per the warranty) would have called for service. Inspection note logged; PM WO drafted but cannot be dispatched under the freeze.

- **CPS:** On site twice for scheduled WOs that pre-dated the freeze. I cannot blame her — they are sized for our MSA volume and we are not feeding the MSA.
