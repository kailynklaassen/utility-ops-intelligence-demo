## 1. Scope of Emergency Work

Apex was dispatched 2025-05-02 at 14:18 PDT after Cascadia Power Services (CPS) reported a >6-hour mobilization estimate. The forced outage was declared on WIN-10058 at 11:52 PDT following a state-of-charge drift alarm cascading into inverter-side under-voltage trip. Scope of authorized emergency work:

- Onsite stabilization and lock-out/tag-out of WIN-10058 at NW-PowerPool-WIND-014.
- Diagnostic sweep of battery management module (BMM), inverter capacitor bank, and DC-link.
- Restoration to service or recommendation of follow-on capital work if restoration not feasible within EPA-2024-11 scope.

Outage duration to restoration: **227 hours**. Lost generation reported by Cascadia SCADA: 2,506.8 MWh.

## 2. Findings

On arrival (2025-05-02 22:40 PDT), Apex crew documented the following:

1. **Inverter capacitor bank C-2** showed measured ESR of 412 mΩ against an OEM allowable of 180 mΩ. Three of nine capacitors visibly bulged; dielectric oil weeping observed at C-2-04 and C-2-07.
2. **Battery management module BMM-A** logged 41 cell-balancing faults in the preceding 14 days, none of which had triggered SCADA alarms above advisory level. Firmware version 3.1.2, last updated 2023-08; current OEM-recommended baseline is 3.4.1.
3. **DC-link bus voltage** ripple measured at 6.8% peak-to-peak against OEM spec of <2.5%. This ripple is the proximate cause of the SoC drift signature observed by the operator.
4. The capacitor bank, BMM, and DC-link form an integrated subsystem on this GE design. In Apex's field judgment, the degradation pattern is **irreversible**: capacitor electrolyte loss has occurred over a multi-year window and the BMM firmware/hardware combination cannot be brought back into OEM tolerance through component-level swaps alone.

## 4. Recommendations

The partial capacitor replacement performed under this emergency authorization will keep WIN-10058 in service for an estimated 90–180 days. However, in Apex's professional judgment:

1. The capacitor bank degradation is symptomatic of broader inverter and battery management module failure. We recommend **full inverter assembly and BMM replacement** within 120 days. Estimated capital scope: $1.05M (separate proposal AP-Q-2025-118 to follow).
2. Continued operation on the partially restored bank will produce repeat SoC drift events. Apex cannot warrant restoration beyond the 90-day window.
3. The DC-link ripple condition is consistent with what GE Service Advisory GE-SA-2022-14 describes as "irreversible degradation" of this generation of inverter platform.
4. We note the firmware version (3.1.2) was overdue for upgrade by more than 18 months. Apex recommends Cascadia review preventive cadence on this platform. (Reference Cascadia internal CAR-2024-118 cited in the dispatch packet.)
