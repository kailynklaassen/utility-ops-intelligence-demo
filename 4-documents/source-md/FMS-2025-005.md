## Section 2 — Significant Events

**12/2 — WIN-10011 efficiency decline outage, NW-PowerPool-WIND-024 (cross-site reference).**
Diego's site. Following on from the 11/23 pre-cursor alert in FMS-2025-004. Duration 111 hours. Lost generation 2,706.6 MWh. Sixth Apex deployment to WIND-024 in 12 months. I am noting it here for continuity even though the primary write-up will be OIR-2025-009.

**12/10, 06:42 PT — TRA-10561 overheating outage, NW-PowerPool-WIND-003.**
This is the event that prompted me to write the next paragraph carefully.

TRA-10561 is the Siemens transformer at the WIND-003 substation. It had a vibration-excess emergency outage on 2025-05-26 — 158 hours, 2,615.1 MWh lost, Apex on site with Siemens Gamesa OEM subcontractors, $446,000 invoiced. That event is documented in OIR-2025-007 and the repair-vs-replace decision was deferred in RAM-2025-001.

On 2025-12-10, at 06:42 PT, the same transformer tripped on bushing overheating. Cooling fan circulation flow loss combined with elevated bushing temperature triggered the protection. Duration 215 hours. Lost generation 3,558.6 MWh. Will be written up as OIR-2025-004 per Engineering's numbering of the year-end events.

Apex was dispatched. The dispatch was the same crew lead, Brad Holstein. The dispatch repaired the same component family (bushings and cooling auxiliary). The invoice has not closed but the field estimate is $670,000–$750,000.

**The same vendor is doing the same emergency repair on the same transformer.** I want that sentence in the record because that is what is happening on the ground. The May repair did not address the underlying condition; it addressed the immediate failure mode. RAM-2025-001 acknowledged the repair-vs-replace question and deferred it. RAM-2025-004 has now been opened to escalate the same question for the second time on the same unit. I recommend immediate engineering review — and I mean the kind that produces a written disposition, not another deferral.

## Section 3 — Vendor Activity

- **Apex:** Two dispatches in December at WIND-003 (TRA-10561 on 12/10, and a follow-up site visit 12/18 to verify cooling auxiliary). Combined estimated $812,000 (not all invoices closed).
- **Apex (cross-site, WIND-024):** WIN-10011 emergency response 12/2. Estimated $640,000.
- **Siemens Energy OEM warranty service:** Reviewed TRA-10561 on 12/15. Their finding was that the bushing replacement done in May "did not include the recommended desiccant cartridge replacement specified in OEM Manual Rev. 3.1, section 8.4." I am not certain that is the whole story — our copy of OEM Manual Rev. 3.1 section 8.4 references an ambient temperature de-rating practice that conflicts with NW-PowerPool's operating profile, and the field crew in May acted on what we believed was a reasonable substitute. There is a documentation conflict here that Engineering needs to resolve.
- **CPS:** Seven scheduled WOs, plus a precautionary visit to WIN-10085 on 12/8 (cooling-loop verification, no findings). Quiet month.
