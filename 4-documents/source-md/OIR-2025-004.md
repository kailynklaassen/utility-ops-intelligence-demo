## 1. Outage Summary

TRA-10561, the main step-up transformer feeding the NW-PowerPool-WIND-003 collector, tripped on top-oil over-temperature on 2025-12-10 at 10:38 PST. Restoration completed 2025-12-19 at 09:38 PST, total duration **215 hours**. Lost generation: **3,558.6 MWh**.

This is the second multi-day outage on TRA-10561 in 2025; the first was a vibration_excess event on 2025-05-26 (OIR-2025-007, 158 h, 2,615.1 MWh). The asset has now logged 8 forced outages and 7,900 MWh of lost generation in FY2025. Cumulative pattern is the subject of RCA-2025-002 (Fall Overheating Sequence) and RCA-2025-005 (procedural shortcut).

## 3. Telemetry Observations

- Top-oil at trip: 99.4 °C (limit 95 per Siemens Energy Manual SET-2200 §4.3; Cascadia internal de-rating policy at NW ambient < 0 °C lowers to 90 °C — pending acceptance, see CAR-2025-005).
- DGA at fault: acetylene 14 ppm, ethylene 84 ppm, methane 41 ppm. Pattern is consistent with localized thermal fault, not arcing.
- Fan bank 3 lock-rotor: fan 3-D motor windings shorted; fans 3-A, 3-B, 3-C operative. The June 2025 PM (WO-2025-06035) recorded "all fans operational" without resistance check on individual motors.
- Ambient: −2 to −7 °C, sustained NW wind 7 m/s.
- Vibration baseline on the transformer tank shows a 12% increase since May 2025 (OIR-2025-007), indicating cumulative core looseness.

## 5. Preliminary Root Cause

Direct cause: cooling fan bank 3 failure under cold-ambient peak-load conditions caused top-oil temperature excursion and DGA-indicated thermal fault.

Contributing causes:
1. Fan-motor resistance check omitted from the June 2025 PM (WO-2025-06035). The CSM-7-equivalent procedure for transformer auxiliaries was not executed; the technician's log notes "deferred to year-end shutdown" — see RCA-2025-005 and CAR-2025-006.
2. Top-oil de-rating policy for sustained cold ambient is documented in Standard MS-2025-03 but not loaded into the SCADA alarm template for TRA-10561. The unit was running to the OEM threshold rather than the internal threshold. See CAR-2025-005.
3. Cumulative core vibration since May (OIR-2025-007) was not actioned in the intervening 6 months — the planned mid-year inspection (WO-2025-08019) was deferred.

## 6. Recommendations

1. Execute fan-motor resistance checks on all NW-PowerPool transformer auxiliaries within 30 days. Track in CAR-2025-008.
2. Load the MS-2025-03 cold-ambient de-rating curve into SCADA alarm templates fleet-wide. Track in CAR-2025-005.
3. Reject Apex's transformer replacement recommendation. Internal engineering plan succeeded; replacement quote at $1.86M not warranted.
4. Add TRA-10561 to FY26 capital planning watchlist; cumulative pattern indicates end-of-economic-life within 2 years.
5. Cross-reference OIR-2025-007 (May vibration event), RCA-2025-002 (Fall Overheating Sequence), RCA-2025-005 (procedural shortcut).
