# Vendor Repair Agreement VRA-2025-001

**Document ID:** VRA-2025-001
**Effective Date:** January 1, 2023
**Renewal Through:** December 31, 2027
**Agreement Type:** Preventive Maintenance Multi-Year Master Services Agreement (MSA)
**Parties:** Cascadia Renewable Operations LLC ("Operator"), a Delaware limited liability company with principal office in Portland, Oregon, and Cascadia Power Services Inc. ("CPS" or "Contractor"), a Washington corporation.
**Governing Standard:** Cascadia Mechanical Standard MS-2025-03 (Rev. February 2025) and predecessor Standard MS-2022-01.
**Distribution:** Procurement; NW-PowerPool Operations; MISO Operations; Internal Audit; CPS Account Manager.

---

## Preamble

This Master Services Agreement was awarded to CPS through competitive bid in Q4 2022 and constitutes Cascadia Renewable Operations' preferred-partner instrument for scheduled preventive maintenance, condition-based monitoring, and non-emergency corrective work across the Northwest Power Pool (NW-PowerPool) and Midcontinent Independent System Operator (MISO) footprints. The Agreement was renewed in October 2024 following a satisfactory three-year performance review.

---

## Section 1 — Scope of Services

1.1 **Authorized Scope.** Contractor shall furnish labor, supervision, tooling, and consumable materials to perform the following categories of work on Operator's wind generation, substation, and balance-of-plant assets within NW-PowerPool and MISO:

   (a) Scheduled preventive maintenance per the Cascadia Preventive Maintenance Cadence Schedule (PMCS-2025), as informed by Standard MS-2025-03;
   (b) Condition-based monitoring including vibration analysis, oil-spectrometer sampling, infrared thermography, and bearing-temperature trending;
   (c) Non-emergency corrective maintenance and refurbishment of rotating equipment, gearboxes, bearings, cooling skids, and switchgear components;
   (d) Component refurbishment in lieu of replacement where mechanically and economically justified.

1.2 **Refurbishment-First Principle.** Contractor acknowledges Operator's preference, codified in Standard MS-2025-03 §4.2, that components be refurbished where doing so restores function to within OEM tolerances at less than 60% of replacement cost.

1.3 **Geographic Coverage.** NW-PowerPool (primary) and MISO (secondary). CAISO and SPP scope is excluded and is the subject of separate agreements (see VRA-2025-003 and VRA-2025-004).

---

## Section 2 — Rate Schedule

2.1 **Hourly Labor Rate.** $185.00 per technician-hour, fully burdened, inclusive of supervision and standard tooling. No call-out minimum.

2.2 **Premium Multipliers.** None. All work under this MSA is scheduled in advance through the joint planning process described in Section 4 and is billed at the straight-time rate.

2.3 **Parts Mark-Up.** Flat 8% on documented invoice cost of parts and consumables, with full pass-through of OEM warranty credits.

2.4 **Travel & Mobilization.** Included in the hourly rate for sites within 250 miles of the Vancouver, WA service hub. Sites beyond 250 miles billed at $0.62/mile and one-way travel time at $92.50/hr (50% of labor rate).

2.5 **Annual Escalation.** CPI-U (West Region), capped at 3.5% per anniversary year.

---

## Section 3 — Service Level Agreements

3.1 **Scheduled Work.** Contractor shall arrive on site within the agreed mobilization window for scheduled work (typically 7–14 days notice).

3.2 **Non-Emergency Corrective Response.** Contractor shall respond to non-emergency corrective work requests within 72 business hours, with on-site presence within 7 calendar days.

3.3 **Reporting.** Contractor shall provide a written field maintenance summary within 5 business days of work completion, referencing the corresponding Cascadia work order (WO-2025-XXXXX format).

3.4 **Emergency Exclusion.** Contractor is not obligated to mobilize inside the 4-hour emergency window defined under EPA-2024-11; emergency activations are governed by VRA-2025-002 (Apex Emergency Grid Solutions).

---

## Section 4 — Approval & Authorization Workflow

4.1 **Standard Work Authorization.** Work orders are issued by Operator's Regional Maintenance Planner and countersigned by Contractor's Account Manager. Joint planning meetings occur monthly.

4.2 **Cost-Threshold Approvals.** Single work orders exceeding $75,000 require approval by the Operator's Regional Director of Operations. Work orders exceeding $250,000 require approval by the Operator's VP, Asset Management.

4.3 **Out-of-Scope Recommendations.** Contractor shall not undertake replacement (as distinct from refurbishment) of major rotating components without a Repair Approval Memo (RAM) issued by Operator. Cross-reference: RAM-2025-001, RAM-2025-002.

4.4 **Interface with Emergency Procurement.** If Contractor cannot mobilize within the 4-hour emergency window per EPA-2024-11, Operator may activate the emergency vendor (Apex Emergency Grid Solutions, VRA-2025-002). Contractor shall be notified in writing within 24 hours of any such activation and shall be afforded the opportunity to assume follow-on remediation scope.

---

## Section 5 — Performance Metrics

5.1 **Tracked Metrics.** Contractor performance is reviewed quarterly against:

   (a) Mean Time to Repair (MTTR) for non-emergency corrective work;
   (b) Preventive maintenance schedule adherence (% of scheduled WOs completed within window);
   (c) Outage-reduction outcomes at sites under Contractor's preventive cadence (year-over-year forced outage rate);
   (d) Documentation completeness against Standard MS-2025-03.

5.2 **Outage-Reduction Performance Bonus.** Contractor is eligible for a calendar-year bonus equal to 4% of total billings if year-over-year forced outage count at NW-PowerPool sites under Contractor's preventive cadence declines by 15% or more. The bonus has been earned in 2023 and 2024; the 2025 calculation is in dispute due to the deferred-PM scope (see CAR-2024-118, CAR-2025-003).

5.3 **Joint Reliability Review.** Operator and Contractor shall hold a semi-annual reliability review. The May 2025 review was deferred and rescheduled to August 2025 in light of the Spring Vibration Cascade events.

---

## Section 6 — Limitations, Exclusions, Termination

6.1 **Exclusions.** This Agreement does not cover:

   (a) OEM-warranty work on Siemens Gamesa equipment (see VRA-2025-005);
   (b) Emergency mobilization inside the EPA-2024-11 window;
   (c) Substation primary equipment manufactured by ABB pre-2018, which is subject to OEM-only service per VTM-ABB-SE7100.

6.2 **Termination for Convenience.** Either party may terminate on 180 days' written notice. Operator retains the right to terminate immediately for cause, including documented failure to meet Section 3 SLAs on three or more consecutive work orders.

6.3 **Indemnification & Insurance.** Standard mutual indemnification. Contractor shall maintain general liability insurance of not less than $10,000,000 per occurrence and professional liability of not less than $5,000,000.

6.4 **Document Hierarchy.** In the event of conflict between this Agreement, Standard MS-2025-03, and an OEM manual, the order of precedence is: (1) applicable regulatory requirement; (2) Standard MS-2025-03; (3) this Agreement; (4) OEM manual. Conflicts are to be escalated through the Management of Change process (see MOC-2025-022 for the open Siemens Gamesa threshold dispute).
