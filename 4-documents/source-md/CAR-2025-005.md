## 1. Finding

ABB Power Grids Substation Service Manual **SE-7100** (filed **VTM-ABB-SE7100**), §6.4 "Recommended Major Maintenance Intervals," specifies a **48-month** (4-year) cycle for medium-voltage breaker overhaul, dielectric oil change, and protective-relay calibration on the 145 kV and 230 kV substation product families deployed at NW-PowerPool sites. Cascadia internal **Standard MS-2025-03 §8.1**, revised February 2025, requires a **24-month** (2-year) cycle for substation assets located in the NW-PowerPool ambient envelope (winter low <-12 °C, summer high >38 °C, salt-fog exposure within 80 km of the coast).

The 4-year ABB cadence is calibrated against an indoor or temperate outdoor environmental envelope (ABB-published reference: ANSI/IEEE C37.06 normal duty). The NW-PowerPool envelope exceeds the manual's stated envelope on three of four environmental axes. MS-2025-03 §8.1 codifies the more aggressive cycle accordingly.

Maintenance execution at SUB-10641 has historically followed the ABB 4-year cadence. The asset's 15 forced outages in FY2025 — the most of any substation in the Cascadia fleet — appear to be partially attributable to under-maintenance against the environmental-adjusted standard.

- OUT-2025-1627 (2025-06-28, efficiency_decline, 176 h, 1,943.6 MWh lost) — relay miscoordination.

- 12 additional smaller events of which 9 cite breaker-mechanism or relay-calibration drift.

### 2.3 Comparable substation performance

- SUB-10629 (Hitachi Energy, NW-PowerPool-WIND-024): 4 forced outages, last service 2023-10 (2-year cycle followed). 9,800 MWh lost (heavily weighted by single OUT-2025-1600 event).
- SUB-10650 (ABB, ERCOT-WIND-039): 6 forced outages, on 4-year ABB cycle but with significantly less aggressive ambient envelope.

The SUB-10641 outage rate is approximately 3.75× the SUB-10629 rate within the same region — both ABB and Hitachi Energy assets, the principal procedural variable being the 4-year vs. 2-year cadence.

### 2.4 ABB warranty interaction

ABB has stated in correspondence dated 2025-07-31 that "service performed by non-ABB-certified technicians on an interval shorter than that recommended in §6.4 does not affect warranty, provided ABB-spec consumables and tools are used." This removes the warranty obstacle to MS-2025-03 compliance. The conflict is therefore not legally constrained; it is operationally and procedurally constrained.

## 3. Root Cause Hypothesis

- **Primary:** Maintenance plans for SUB-10641 were built in 2017 against SE-7100 §6.4 and have not been retro-fitted to the MS-2025-03 §8.1 standard issued in February 2025.
- **Contributing:** The maintenance-planning team at NW-PowerPool defaults to OEM-manual cadence unless an explicit engineering memo directs otherwise. No such memo was issued to apply MS-2025-03 §8.1 to the existing substation fleet.
- **Contributing:** The 2025 scheduled overhaul was deferred under the budget freeze (CAR-2025-001), removing the natural opportunity to re-baseline the cadence.

## 4. Conflict With Existing Guidance

- **VTM-ABB-SE7100 §6.4** — 4-year cycle.
- **Cascadia Standard MS-2025-03 §8.1** — 2-year cycle for NW-PowerPool ambient envelope.
- **IEEE C37.10.1-2018 §5.2** (industry consensus standard, breaker condition-based maintenance) — supports environmental-adjusted cadence; references temperature cycling factors that align with MS-2025-03.

This conflict, unlike CAR-2025-002 (Siemens Gamesa), does not have warranty implications. It is purely a cost-and-cadence conflict where the OEM manual recommends less maintenance than internal standards require.

## 5. Recommended Corrective Action

1. Issue **Substation Maintenance Plan Update SMP-2025-04** retro-fitting MS-2025-03 §8.1 cadence to all NW-PowerPool ABB and Hitachi Energy substation assets effective 2026-Q1.
2. Schedule immediate (within 90 days of CAR re-issue) interim service at SUB-10641 covering breaker timing, oil DGA, and relay calibration. Estimated scope: $148K (CPS quote, dated 2025-08-30).
3. Add SUB-10641 to the Engineering Review list (cross-reference CAR-2025-009 — escalation policy non-compliance).
4. Update Maintenance Engineering training to clarify that OEM-manual cadence is a **floor**, not a ceiling, when MS-2025-03 specifies more frequent intervals.
5. Add an Engineering co-sign requirement to any maintenance plan that uses OEM-manual default cadence on Tier-1 substation assets.
