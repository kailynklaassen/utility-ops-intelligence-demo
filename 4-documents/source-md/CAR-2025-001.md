## 1. Finding

A formal review of Computerized Maintenance Management System (CMMS) work-order ledgers covering the period 2025-01-06 through 2025-03-31 confirms that fourteen (14) preventive maintenance (PM) work orders scheduled at NW-PowerPool-WIND-014 and NW-PowerPool-WIND-024 were administratively deferred under the Q1 2025 capital-and-operating expense freeze (Finance Directive FD-2025-002, dated 2024-12-19). Of the 14 deferred PMs, 11 were rescheduled past their Cascadia Standard MS-2025-03 maintenance-window tolerance (±30 days). Three were closed out as "completed by rescheduling" without on-site service being performed.

The deferred work scope included bearing vibration spectral analysis, gearbox oil-spectrometer sampling, and substation breaker timing tests — the precise failure modes observed during the May–June 2025 Spring Vibration Cascade.

## 3. Root Cause Hypothesis

- **Primary:** Finance Directive FD-2025-002 imposed an undifferentiated freeze across capital and operating budget categories. PM execution at NW-PowerPool was funded through the operating budget; no exception process existed for safety- or reliability-critical PMs.
- **Contributing:** The CMMS deferral workflow allowed deferral approval at the site-supervisor level, with no escalation gate to Engineering for assets on the "watch list" (WIN-10016 had been added to the watch list in 2024 — see CAR-2024-118).
- **Contributing:** No reliability-risk score was attached to deferred PMs at the time of approval. The system did not flag that 11 of the 14 deferrals were on assets with three or more forced outages in the trailing twelve months.

## 4. Conflict With Existing Guidance

- The deferrals violate **Cascadia Standard MS-2025-03 §4.3**, which states PM intervals on Tier-2 (watch-list) assets shall not exceed +30 days from scheduled date without Engineering concurrence.
- The deferrals are consistent in form (but not substance) with **Finance Directive FD-2025-002**, which contained no carve-out for reliability-critical work.
- Action **CAR-2024-118** (issued December 2024, "Preventive Maintenance Cadence Inadequate For Aging Wind Assets") had already flagged WIN-10016 and SUB-10641 as inadequately serviced. CAR-2024-118 remains open.

## 5. Recommended Corrective Action

1. **Issue Standing Exception SE-2025-014** to Finance Directive FD-2025-002 covering all Tier-1 and Tier-2 reliability-critical PMs. Exception authority to rest with the Director of Reliability, not site supervisors.
2. Modify CMMS deferral workflow to require Engineering co-sign for any PM deferral on an asset with two or more forced outages in trailing 12 months. Hard system gate, not advisory.
3. Execute the eight (8) deferred WOs that were "never rescheduled" within 60 days of CAR re-issue. List in §6 below.
4. Reconcile WO-2025-00472 and WO-2025-00473 (oil-spectrometer samples) against the ambiguity flagged in CAR-2025-003 — sample frequency for CSM-7 differs between CPS and Apex execution.
5. Add a recurring quarterly Reliability & Compliance review of "administratively closed" PMs (the three orders closed without on-site work being performed).
