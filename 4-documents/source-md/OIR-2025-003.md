## 1. Outage Summary

WIN-10085 declared a forced outage on 2025-10-07 at 07:07 PDT after a gearbox over-temperature trip. Return to service occurred on 2025-10-16 at 17:07 PDT, total duration **226 hours**. Lost generation: **3,740.6 MWh**. This is the opening event of what Reliability Engineering has begun referring to as the **Fall Overheating Sequence** (October–December 2025), formally classified under RCA-2025-002.

WIN-10085 is a Vestas-supplied unit. Vestas fleet history at MISO-WIND-038 has been the lowest-failure baseline in the Cascadia portfolio, which makes this event notable as a deviation rather than a fleet trend. The trip mechanism is consistent with a maintenance-side cause rather than an OEM design issue.

## 3. Telemetry Observations

- Gearbox oil sump at trip: 89.7 °C.
- Main bearing at trip: 94.1 °C.
- Coolant flow rate at trip: 41 L/min (rated 64 L/min).
- Iron particulate in oil: 8 ppm — within limits, supporting the heat-exchanger fouling hypothesis.
- Vibration RMS: 4.2 mm/s (well below MS-2025-03 limit 6.5).
- Heat-exchanger fin spacing audit: 11 of 24 channels >50% blocked with fine particulate; consistent with the 2025-08-09 through 2025-08-21 regional wildfire smoke event (NW-PowerPool ambient AQI sustained >180).

The cooling system inspection scheduled for September under WO-2025-09081 was closed "complete, no findings" by Pinnacle Energy Maintenance. Photo evidence attached to that WO does not include heat-exchanger fin images. See CAR-2025-004 and CAR-2025-007.

## 5. Preliminary Root Cause

Direct cause: heat-exchanger fin fouling from wildfire smoke particulate, leading to reduced coolant flow and runaway sump temperature under sustained 7.4 MW load.

Contributing causes:
1. Cooling system inspection WO-2025-09081 was closed without verifying the heat-exchanger fins. Pinnacle Energy Maintenance documentation deficiency — see CAR-2025-004.
2. There is no written Cascadia procedure for post-wildfire-smoke inspection. The OEM manual (Vestas Service Procedure VSP-3.2) does not contemplate sustained particulate exposure; the gap should be addressed in CSM-7. See CAR-2025-007.

## 6. Recommendations

1. Issue a one-time inspection sweep of heat-exchanger fins at all NW-PowerPool wind assets within 30 days. Track under WO-2025-10444 (in preparation).
2. Add post-particulate-event inspection to CSM-7. Refer to CAR-2025-007.
3. Audit Pinnacle Energy Maintenance close-out documentation across NW-PowerPool-WIND-003 and -024 for the September 2025 PM cycle. Refer to CAR-2025-004.
4. Add WIN-10085 cooling system to FY26 routine refurbishment schedule. Internal estimate $185K per turbine.
5. Cross-reference RCA-2025-002 for the broader Fall Overheating Sequence and OIR-2025-004 (TRA-10561, same site, December event).
