## 1. Scope of Emergency Work

Apex was dispatched at 03:11 PDT 2025-05-26 in response to a vibration_excess trip on TRA-10561. CPS confirmed inability to mobilize a transformer-qualified crew within 4 hours due to overlapping deployments in MISO. EPA-2024-11 was invoked by the on-call duty manager.

Authorized scope:
- Onsite isolation, oil sampling, and acoustic survey of TRA-10561.
- Stabilization and return to service if achievable within emergency window.
- OEM-coordinated assessment to identify root mechanical cause.

Outage duration to partial restoration: **158 hours**. Lost generation per Cascadia SCADA: 2,615.1 MWh.

## 2. Findings

Onsite arrival 2025-05-26 08:34 PDT.

1. **Vibration spectrum at the LV bushing turret** showed dominant 120 Hz and 360 Hz components at 18.4 mm/s and 11.2 mm/s respectively — well above the ISO 10816-3 zone C/D boundary of 7.1 mm/s for this class.
2. **Dissolved gas analysis (DGA)** of the main tank oil returned acetylene 14 ppm, ethylene 92 ppm, hydrogen 280 ppm. The acetylene reading is indicative of recent arcing; the IEEE C57.104 ratio places the unit in "Condition 3 — investigate immediately."
3. **Acoustic emission survey** localized partial discharge activity to the HV winding outboard end, phase B, suggesting inter-turn insulation degradation.
4. The unit's most recent **oil-spectrometer test** on file was dated 2024-04-11. Per OEM Siemens Energy Transformer Manual SE-TR-2300 §7.4, oil-spectrometer testing on this class is **annual**. The CSM-7 internal procedure (Cascadia) lists this step as quarterly, but the procedure note on the asset history reads "performed annually per OEM guidance" — which is the cadence Apex followed during prior service visits and is consistent with OEM documentation provided to us. (Cascadia engineering may wish to reconcile internal CSM-7 to OEM Siemens Manual SE-TR-2300.)
5. Mechanical assessment indicates winding clamping force has decayed; the 120 Hz signature is consistent with loosened core-laminations exciting the LV winding.

## 4. Recommendations

The temporary winding restraint and oil change will keep TRA-10561 in service in a derated configuration (60 MVA continuous, 65 MVA 30-minute). In Apex's professional judgment:

1. The HV winding inter-turn insulation degradation is unlikely to recover. We recommend **full HV and LV winding replacement** within 180 days. Estimated capital scope: $1.45M (separate proposal AP-Q-2025-129 to follow).
2. CSM-7 oil-spectrometer testing should be confirmed as annual per OEM guidance; the conflicting quarterly cadence in some Cascadia internal documents appears to be a residual from a predecessor procedure and is not supported by Siemens Energy.
3. Vibration monitoring at the LV bushing turret should be elevated from advisory to action-level above 7.1 mm/s consistent with ISO 10816-3.
4. Apex recommends an OEM-supervised factory acceptance test for whichever replacement option is selected.
