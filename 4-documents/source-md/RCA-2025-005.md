## Executive Summary

Audit of close-out documentation for CSM-7 (Cooling System Maintenance Procedure) work orders at NW-PowerPool in FY2025 found that the **quarterly oil-spectrometer step was bypassed or deferred on 11 of 42 work orders** (26%). The bypasses cluster around two periods: the Spring Vibration Cascade (RCA-2025-001, May–June) and the run-up to the Fall Overheating Sequence (RCA-2025-002, July–September preparation window).

The bypass pattern is implicated as a contributing factor in at least two major outages:

1. **OIR-2025-002** — WIN-10131 August overheating event. CSM-7 quarterly oil-spectrometer test scheduled for July 2025 (WO-2025-07212) was closed as "deferred to next scheduled outage" per technician notes. Iron particulate at the time of the August trip was 18 ppm (limit 12).
2. **OIR-2025-004** — TRA-10561 December overheating event. The fan-motor resistance check (analogous transformer auxiliary procedure) was omitted from the June 2025 PM (WO-2025-06035), recorded as "deferred to year-end shutdown." Fan bank 3-D failed under load.

This RCA does not assign individual blame. The bypass pattern is a **predictable response to fatigue and pressure** under the FMP-2 violations documented in CAR-2025-001 and to the staffing shortfalls during Spring Vibration Cascade response. The corrective action is **procedural and organizational, not disciplinary.**

## 1. Failure Mode Classification

**H-1 (Human-Procedural):** Technicians, faced with a multi-hour PM that includes a 90-minute oil-spectrometer step requiring shutdown of the unit and offline lab analysis, defer the step when the schedule is compressed. The procedure permits this in writing — CSM-7 §3.6 allows deferral "with written justification" — but in practice the written justifications are perfunctory or absent.

**O-1 (Organizational):** The factors driving compression are organizational: surge response demands during outage clusters, FMP-2 fatigue cap exceedances, single-depot dispatch constraints (referenced in RCA-2025-001 and RCA-2025-004). The procedure-bypass is a symptom of an over-loaded delivery system.

## 2. Fishbone / Contributing Factors

**Method:**
- CSM-7 §3.6 deferral language is permissive. The procedure permits deferral with "written justification by lead technician." It does not require management approval or backlog tracking.
- There is no formal CSM-7-deferral dashboard. Deferrals are recorded in technician notes and discovered only on audit.

**Manpower:**
- FMP-2 cap was exceeded by 4 lead techs during Spring Vibration Cascade (CAR-2025-001). Lead techs were drawn from the same pool that executes quarterly CSM-7 work, creating direct competition between firefighting and PM.
- Cross-training on the oil-spectrometer instrument is limited; 6 of 22 NW-PowerPool technicians are certified. Bench depth is insufficient.

**Method (training):**
- The 2024 CSM-7 refresher training included a module on "when to defer" but did not include a module on "deferral cost and downstream risk." The training implicitly normalized deferral.

**Machine:**
- The oil-spectrometer instrument at the Bend depot was out-of-calibration from 2025-03-15 to 2025-05-02. During that window, samples were sent to the regional offsite lab; turnaround increased from 4 hours to 6 days. This significantly extended the time-cost of executing the step and contributed to deferral.

**Materials:**
- Pinnacle Energy Maintenance close-out templates for CSM-7-equivalent work do not include an explicit "oil-spectrometer executed Y/N" field. Pinnacle close-outs at OIR-2025-001, OIR-2025-003, and OIR-2025-009 had missing or ambiguous oil-spectrometer entries. See CAR-2025-004.

## 5. Long-Term Corrective Recommendations

1. **Amend CSM-7 §3.6** to require:
   - Management approval (Plant Manager or designee) for any deferral.
   - A specific re-scheduling date within 30 days of deferral.
   - Backlog tracking of all CSM-7 deferrals in the operations dashboard.
2. **Expand technician certification** on oil-spectrometer operation. Target 12 certified technicians at NW-PowerPool by end of Q2 2026.
3. **Recalibrate and add a second oil-spectrometer** at the Bend depot to remove the calibration-window single-point-of-failure. Internal estimate $42K.
4. **Pinnacle Energy Maintenance close-out template** must include explicit Y/N fields for each CSM-7 sub-step. Refer to CAR-2025-004 and VRA-2025-004.
5. **Address upstream organizational drivers** — FMP-2 enforcement (CAR-2025-001), single-depot footprint (RCA-2025-004 recommendation 5), OEM-bulletin intake SLA (CAR-2025-009). The procedural shortcut is downstream of these.
6. **Training module update.** Replace "when to defer" with "deferral cost and downstream risk" in the 2026 CSM-7 refresher.
7. **No individual discipline.** This RCA explicitly recommends against treating documented deferrals as performance issues. The structural causes are organizational; punishing individual technicians will reduce documentation quality further, which is the opposite of what is needed.

## 6. Vendor Performance Commentary

**Pinnacle Energy Maintenance** is the primary external contractor for CSM-7 work at NW-PowerPool-WIND-003 and -024. Their close-out template inherits from a pre-acquisition predecessor company (acquired 2022) and references deprecated procedure numbers. This documentation drift is independent of the deferral pattern but compounds it — when Pinnacle close-outs are ambiguous, deferral becomes harder to detect. Refer to CAR-2025-004.

**Cascadia Power Services Inc.** internal CSM-7 close-outs are higher quality but not perfect. CPS does record oil-spectrometer results in a structured field; the gap is in deferral justification, which is free-text.

**Heartland Reliability Partners** has not been used at NW-PowerPool for CSM-7 work. The Heartland MSA includes a structured close-out template that could be a model for the Pinnacle redline.
