## 1. Scope of Emergency Work

Apex was dispatched at 04:02 PST 2025-11-12 in response to a state-of-charge drift trip at SUB-10629, the same substation interconnect on which Apex performed work earlier in FY2025 (reference OUT-2025-1601 of 2025-04-11; cross-reference EMR-2025-001-class invoicing). CPS reported a 5-hour mobilization estimate; EPA-2024-11 was invoked.

**This is the 5th Apex deployment to NW-PowerPool-WIND-024 in the trailing 12 months** (prior: 2024-12, 2025-04, 2025-08 on WIN-10131, 2025-08 substation auxiliary). Apex acknowledges the cumulative pattern and includes this note for Cascadia vendor management awareness (cross-reference CAR-2025-004).

Authorized scope:
- Onsite isolation of SUB-10629, BMS diagnostic, and protection-relay re-coordination.
- Stabilization, return to service, and OEM-coordinated assessment.

Outage duration to restoration: **107 hours**. Lost generation per Cascadia SCADA: 2,609.0 MWh.

## 2. Findings

Onsite arrival 2025-11-12 12:18 PST.

1. **BMS Bank B-3** — the same bank that drove the April event (OUT-2025-1601) — returned a fresh SoC drift signature. Firmware was upgraded to 3.4.1 in the April scope; however, control board CB-7 returned thermal alarm 23 minutes prior to trip. Thermal imaging confirmed CB-7 surface temperature 84 °C against allowable 55 °C.
2. **Hitachi Energy Service Bulletin SB-HE-2025-04** (published 2025-09) advises replacement of CB-7 control boards manufactured in production batches 2021-Q3 through 2022-Q1 due to a known capacitor selection defect. SUB-10629's CB-7 is in batch 2022-Q1. The bulletin applies.
3. **Protection relay R-12B** operated correctly. Re-coordination required after CB-7 swap.
4. **DGA on the adjacent transformer auxiliary** within tolerance — no spillover concern.

## 4. Recommendations

The CB-7 swap addresses the known-defect condition. In Apex's professional judgment:

1. **Cascadia should authorize OEM-supervised substation board refresh** for the full Bank B-3 control assembly (5 boards beyond CB-7) within 90 days, to forestall recurrence. The bulletin is specific to CB-7 batch 2022-Q1, but the broader control assembly is the same vintage and design generation. Estimated capital scope: $385K (Hitachi quote HE-Q-2025-088 to follow).
2. Apex notes the repeat-failure pattern on Bank B-3 at SUB-10629 across April and November 2025. The proximate causes were different (firmware then; control-board hardware now), but the asset has experienced 2 SoC-drift trips in 7 months and warrants asset-level review.
3. We are also aware Cascadia's CAR-2025-002 calls for accelerated PM cadence at NW-PowerPool-WIND-024. Apex supports that direction.
