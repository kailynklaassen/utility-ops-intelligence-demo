## 1. Finding

Apex Emergency Grid Solutions has been activated under **EPA-2024-11** ("Emergency Procurement Authorization — November 2024") eleven (11) times in FY2025. The master agreement supporting EPA-2024-11 (Vendor Repair Agreement VRA-2024-007, executed 2024-11-22) was constructed as a sole-source mobilization vehicle to address the deficiency in CPS off-hours coverage identified in CAR-2024-094. As executed, VRA-2024-007 contains:

- Time-and-materials pricing only ($420/hr base labor; 1.5× weekend multiplier; 35% emergency parts markup).
- No fixed-price scope offerings.
- No performance criteria (no MTTR target, no first-time-fix rate, no warranty period on emergency repair work).
- No scope ceiling per activation.
- No competitive-rebid clause; renewal is auto-extending on annual basis absent active termination.

The eleven FY2025 Apex invoices average **$385K** per activation. Two CPS scope estimates for equivalent work (provided in response to RCA-2025-001 cost-benchmarking request and post-hoc analysis of OUT-2025-1601) average **$96K** for the same line items. The differential is approximately **4×**. Of the **$8.9M** in NW-PowerPool emergency vendor spend in FY2025, Apex and Apex-subcontracted Siemens Gamesa OEM service account for the entirety.

### 2.2 Cost benchmarking

For the seven activations where Engineering overrode Apex's replacement recommendation and proceeded with refurbishment via CPS or in-house, the realized cost averaged **$96,300** including parts, against the Apex quoted scope. The Apex-vs-CPS ratio on like-for-like work is **3.99×**.

Apex's published labor rate of $420/hr (1.5× on weekends) compares to CPS at $185/hr flat. The hourly differential alone is 2.27×; the remaining differential (1.75×) reflects parts markup (35% vs. 8%) and scope inflation (Apex consistently quotes one tier of intervention higher than CPS).

### 2.3 First-time-fix data

Of the 11 Apex activations, four (APX-2025-001, -003, -005, -009) were followed by another forced outage on the same asset within 90 days. First-time-fix rate by this measure is approximately 64%. No contractual remedy exists because no first-time-fix obligation was written into VRA-2024-007.

## 3. Root Cause Hypothesis

- **Primary:** VRA-2024-007 was negotiated under time pressure in November 2024 to close a CPS off-hours coverage gap. The drafting Procurement team prioritized speed of mobilization over scope or pricing discipline. Legal review at the time noted (memo dated 2024-11-15) that "performance terms are minimal; recommend revisit at first renewal." No revisit occurred.
- **Contributing:** Apex sub-contracts Siemens Gamesa OEM technicians at 1.8× labor rate for OEM-warranty-bearing work. This relationship is not disclosed in VRA-2024-007 and creates a financial alignment between Apex's revenue and Siemens Gamesa's replacement-recommendation posture (cross-reference CAR-2025-002).
- **Contributing:** EPA-2024-11 mobilization standard (<4 hours) is satisfied only by Apex in NW-PowerPool, structurally preventing competitive sourcing for emergencies in that region.

## 4. Conflict With Existing Guidance

- **Cascadia Procurement Policy PP-2023-04** §7.2 requires all master agreements >$1M annualized spend to include performance criteria and rebid cadence. VRA-2024-007 violates this. The EPA-2024-11 sole-source justification was used to bypass §7.2.
- The EPA mechanism is designed for individual transactions; using it to perpetuate a sole-source relationship across an entire fiscal year arguably violates the intent of EPA-2024-11 §3.1 ("emergency-only, transactional basis").
- **CAR-2024-094** (off-hours coverage gap) is the predicate document for VRA-2024-007. Its remedy was the master agreement; the master agreement has itself become the new finding.

## 5. Recommended Corrective Action

1. Convert VRA-2024-007 from time-and-materials to a **performance-based emergency services contract** at next renewal (2025-11-22 already passed; auto-renewal occurred). Negotiate an early renegotiation effective 2026-Q2.
2. Insert pricing caps per activation: not-to-exceed $250K without procurement co-sign; not-to-exceed $750K without VP Operations sign-off.
3. Insert performance criteria: minimum 90% first-time-fix rate (90-day reoutage window), MTTR target 24 h for substations / 48 h for wind drivetrain, 12-month workmanship warranty on emergency repair.
4. Issue RFP for a second emergency vendor in NW-PowerPool region to break sole-source. Heartland Reliability Partners has expressed interest (cross-reference VRA-2025-XXX, draft).
5. Require Apex to disclose subcontractor labor (Siemens Gamesa OEM technicians) and apply Cascadia's no-related-party-markup rule.
6. Establish a quarterly emergency-spend governance review at the CFO level.

**Open.** Renegotiation notice has not been issued. Apex was paid $352K on activation APX-2025-011 (December 2025) under unmodified terms.
