## 1. Outage Summary

WIN-10131 declared a forced outage on 2025-01-13 at 20:19 PST on a gearbox high-temperature trip. The asset returned to service on 2025-01-19 at 15:19 PST, total duration **139 hours**. Lost generation: **3,389.3 MWh**. This is the first forced-outage event of FY2025 at NW-PowerPool-WIND-024 and the opening event in what later becomes a repeated WIN-10131 overheating pattern (see OIR-2025-002 for the August event).

This report is also the first FY2025 document to flag the conflict between Siemens Gamesa OEM Manual Rev. 4.2 and Cascadia internal Standard MS-2025-03, later formalized in MOC-2025-022.

## 3. Telemetry Observations

- Gearbox oil sump at trip: 91.2 °C.
- Main bearing at trip: 95.8 °C.
- Vibration RMS: 6.9 mm/s on main shaft — above MS-2025-03 6.5 limit, below OEM Manual Rev. 4.2 10.0 threshold.
- Iron particulate: 14 ppm — first exceedance of 12 ppm limit on this unit since commissioning.
- Ambient: −4 to −9 °C. NW wind 12–18 m/s. Conditions within seasonal norm.
- The Q4 2024 quarterly oil-spectrometer test (WO-2024-12118) had been postponed twice and was outstanding at the time of the event; it was finally executed retrospectively during this outage. See CAR-2024-118.

## 5. Preliminary Root Cause

Direct cause: oil contamination (iron particulate at 14 ppm) reducing lubrication efficacy under cold-soak conditions.

Contributing causes:
1. Q4 2024 quarterly oil-spectrometer test was deferred under CSM-7. Had it been executed on schedule (December 2024), the particulate trend would have been visible 4–6 weeks earlier.
2. NW-PowerPool entered Q1 2025 with 14 preventive WOs on a deferred list against NW-PowerPool-WIND-014 and -024 — Q1 budget freeze. WO-2025-01044 (this unit's January PM) was among them.
3. Documentation conflict: OEM Manual Rev. 4.2 permits continued operation up to 88 °C bearing; Standard MS-2025-03 requires shutdown ≥ 80 °C sustained > 30 min. The operating staff defaulted to the OEM threshold absent a formal Cascadia override. This is the originating procedural conflict that drives MOC-2025-022.

## 6. Recommendations

1. Execute the deferred WO-2025-01044 and the remaining 13 Q1 deferred items as the freeze lifts. Track under CAR-2025-002 (Q1 budget freeze recovery plan).
2. Open a Management of Change request to formally override the OEM threshold or formally adopt it as Cascadia's controlling threshold. Tracked as MOC-2025-022 (subsequently opened 2025-03-04).
3. Audit CSM-7 oil-spectrometer compliance across NW-PowerPool. See CAR-2025-006 (later opened).
4. Add WIN-10131 to the high-watch list; this is the first overheating event of the year and the pattern warrants surveillance.
5. Cross-reference CAR-2024-118 (preventive maintenance cadence inadequate for aging wind assets, open since December 2024).
