## 1. Outage Summary

SUB-10629 declared a forced outage on 2025-11-12 at 03:42 PST on protection action triggered by SoC drift on collector battery buffer Bank B-3. The asset returned to service on 2025-11-16 at 14:42 PST, total duration **107 hours**. Lost generation: **2,609.0 MWh**.

This is the second SoC-drift outage on SUB-10629 in FY2025; the April event is documented in OIR-2025-001. The event sits in the middle of the Fall Overheating Sequence (October–November); RCA-2025-002 traces the broader pattern.

## 3. Telemetry Observations

- SoC drift at trip: 3.2% (advisory 2.0%, action 2.5% per MS-2025-03 §6.2).
- Bus voltage on 34.5 kV side dropped from 34.1 kV to 32.0 kV in final 75 seconds.
- Cell temperature spread within Bank B-3: 14.4 °C (normal < 5).
- TMB Rev. A fault: voltage reference drift on the temperature sensor input — Hitachi Service Bulletin SB-HE-2025-04 (issued July 2025) recommended proactive replacement with Rev. B for assets > 18 months in service. SUB-10629's TMB was 28 months in service.
- Hitachi SB-HE-2025-04 was logged in the Cascadia OEM bulletin intake but no work order was generated. See CAR-2025-009 (OEM bulletin intake-to-WO gap).

## 5. Preliminary Root Cause

Direct cause: TMB Rev. A voltage-reference drift on the temperature sensor input, causing the BMS to mis-balance Bank B-3 and propagating SoC drift to the protection scheme.

Contributing causes:
1. Hitachi Service Bulletin SB-HE-2025-04 (July 2025) was logged in the OEM bulletin intake but no proactive WO was generated. Eight other NW-PowerPool TMBs are in the same > 18-month-in-service population. See CAR-2025-009.
2. Concurrent alarm pressure on the operator. T. Okafor was triaging both this asset and WIN-10011 trending data. CAR-2025-001 (FMP-2 fatigue) and CAR-2025-006 (CSM-7 procedural shortcut) are relevant.

## 6. Recommendations

1. Mobilize fleet-wide TMB Rev. A replacement at NW-PowerPool within 60 days. Track under CAR-2025-009.
2. Formalize a 30-day SLA on OEM service bulletin intake-to-WO. Track under CAR-2025-009.
3. Document this event's cost benchmark ($142,600 with CPS) against OIR-2025-001 ($450,500 with Apex) for vendor MSA renegotiation. Refer to EMR-2025-006.
4. Cross-reference OIR-2025-001 (April SUB-10629 event), OIR-2025-009 (December WIN-10011 event 20 days later) and RCA-2025-002 (Fall Overheating Sequence).
