## 1. Outage Summary

A forced outage was declared at NW-PowerPool-WIND-024 on the SUB-10629 substation interconnect on 2025-04-11 at 17:43 PDT. The asset remained offline until 2025-04-19 at 20:43 PDT, for a duration of **195 hours**. Lost generation during the event was **4,754.8 MWh**, the largest single-asset NW-PowerPool event of Q2 2025. Estimated revenue impact: $285,288.

The event was triggered by a state-of-charge (SoC) drift alarm on the co-located battery buffer feeding the 34.5 kV collector. SoC drift propagated to the substation protection relays, which tripped the interconnect on under-voltage. The asset has now suffered 4 forced outages in FY2025 (refer to repeat-offender summary appended to RCA-2025-002).

## 3. Telemetry Observations

- SoC drift trended from baseline 0.4% (rolling 30-day) to 3.9% over 3 hours and 41 minutes prior to trip. Threshold for "action required" under Standard MS-2025-03 is 2.0% sustained > 30 min.
- Bus voltage on the 34.5 kV side dropped from 34.2 kV to 31.8 kV in the final 90 seconds before the trip.
- Bank B-3 cell temperature spread widened from 4.1 °C to 11.7 °C between adjacent modules, indicating BMS balancing failure.
- Ambient temperature 6 °C; gusting NW wind 19–24 m/s. Conditions within seasonal norm.
- The same drift signature was logged on Bank B-3 on 2025-02-19 and 2025-03-04 without trip — the WO-2025-04118 inspection at the end of February was closed as "PM completed, no anomalies" by Pinnacle Energy Maintenance. See documentation conflict noted in CAR-2025-004.

## 5. Preliminary Root Cause

Battery Management System firmware on Bank B-3 was running version 2.7.1, scheduled for upgrade to 2.7.4 under the Q1 2025 preventive maintenance plan. The upgrade WO (WO-2025-02041) was one of the 14 preventive items deferred under the Q1 2025 budget freeze (see CAR-2024-118 and CAR-2025-002). Version 2.7.1 has a known cell-balancing defect under cold-soak conditions documented in Hitachi Energy Service Bulletin SB-HE-2024-09.

The immediate technical cause was BMS firmware defect. The procedural cause was deferral of a known-corrective firmware update.
