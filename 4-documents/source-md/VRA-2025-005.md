# Vendor Repair Agreement VRA-2025-005

**Document ID:** VRA-2025-005
**Effective Date:** April 1, 2021 (original); Addendum A appended March 10, 2026
**Term:** Initial 5-year warranty + 5-year Extended Service Contract through March 31, 2031
**Agreement Type:** OEM Warranty and Extended Service Contract
**Parties:** Cascadia Renewable Operations LLC ("Operator") and Siemens Gamesa Renewable Energy A/S ("Siemens Gamesa" or "OEM"), a Danish corporation with U.S. service operations headquartered in Orlando, Florida.
**Governing Documents:** Siemens Gamesa Wind Turbine Service Manual, Revision 4.2 (June 2024) — VTM-SIEMENS-GAMESA-4-2; Operator Standard MS-2025-03 (in conflict; see Addendum A).
**Distribution:** Procurement; CAISO Operations; NW-PowerPool Operations; Engineering; Internal Audit; Siemens Gamesa Service Director.

---

## Preamble

This Agreement establishes Operator's OEM service relationship with Siemens Gamesa for the Siemens Gamesa-manufactured wind turbines in Operator's fleet, comprising the initial five-year OEM warranty (commissioned in 2021) and a continuous five-year Extended Service Contract through March 2031.

**Assets Covered.** This Agreement applies to the following Siemens Gamesa-manufactured wind turbines:

| Asset Tag | Site | Region |
|---|---|---|
| WIN-10130 | CAISO-WIND-026 | CAISO |
| WIN-10054 | CAISO-WIND-026 | CAISO |
| WIN-10131 | NW-PowerPool-WIND-024 | NW-PowerPool |
| WIN-10011 | NW-PowerPool-WIND-024 | NW-PowerPool |

These four units account for a disproportionate share of Operator's FY2025 forced-outage MWh and emergency-invoice spend (see OIR-2025-001, OIR-2025-003, OIR-2025-007).

---

## Section 1 — Scope of Services

1.1 **OEM Service Scope.** OEM shall provide:

   (a) Major-component diagnostic, repair, and replacement services for the covered turbines, performed in accordance with Siemens Gamesa Wind Turbine Service Manual, Revision 4.2 (VTM-SIEMENS-GAMESA-4-2);
   (b) Genuine Siemens Gamesa replacement parts and OEM-certified consumables;
   (c) OEM warranty administration for the initial 5-year warranty period and the Extended Service Contract;
   (d) Coordination with Operator's preventive partner (Cascadia Power Services Inc. — see VRA-2025-001) for routine non-major-component work that does not require OEM presence.

1.2 **Subcontract Authority.** OEM service technicians may be subcontracted to Apex Emergency Grid Solutions under VRA-2025-002 §1.3 when emergency mobilization is required inside the EPA-2024-11 four-hour window.

1.3 **Procedural Authority.** OEM service work performed under this Agreement is governed by VTM-SIEMENS-GAMESA-4-2. **Addendum A acknowledges a known conflict between VTM-SIEMENS-GAMESA-4-2 and Operator Standard MS-2025-03 with respect to gearbox vibration replacement thresholds.** See Addendum A and MOC-2025-022.

## Section 2 — Rate Schedule

2.1 **OEM Labor Rate.** $310.00 per OEM technician-hour for in-warranty work (covered at no cost to Operator for warranty-eligible defects); $385.00 per OEM technician-hour for out-of-warranty work and Extended Service Contract work.

2.2 **Premium Multipliers.**
   (a) Weekend / holiday: 1.45× base rate;
   (b) Subcontracted-emergency activation (through VRA-2025-002): 1.8× pass-through to Apex, plus Apex's coordination labor.

| Component | OEM Catalog Price (per unit) |

| Yaw drive motor (full) | $58,000 |

## Section 3 — Service Level Agreements

3.1 **Major-Component Repair Mobilization.** OEM technicians on site within 72 hours of formal Operator request for major-component diagnostic or repair.

3.2 **Emergency Mobilization.** Where the EPA-2024-11 four-hour window applies, mobilization is via subcontract through VRA-2025-002 (Apex).

3.3 **Parts Lead Times.** Major-component parts lead times per OEM catalog. Gearbox assemblies: 14–20 weeks. Generator assemblies: 12–18 weeks. Bearing kits: 4–8 weeks.

---

## Section 4 — Approval & Authorization Workflow

4.1 **Routine Warranty Claims.** OEM warranty claims for covered defects are processed directly between OEM and Operator's Regional Maintenance Planner.

4.2 **Replacement-Class Recommendations.** Any OEM recommendation for replacement of a major component (gearbox, generator, main bearing, blade) must be reviewed against the procedural-conflict mechanism in Section 4.3 and authorized through a Repair Approval Memo (RAM).

4.3 **Procedural Conflict Mechanism (Addendum A).** Where the OEM's recommendation is based on a VTM-SIEMENS-GAMESA-4-2 threshold that conflicts with Operator Standard MS-2025-03, the recommendation shall be flagged in writing, routed to Operator's Engineering function under MOC-2025-022, and held pending Engineering disposition before any capital is committed. The most material example as of the date of this revision is the gearbox vibration replacement threshold (see Addendum A §A.1).

4.4 **EPA-2024-11 Interaction.** Where OEM service is delivered via subcontract under VRA-2025-002, the activation authorization travels through the EPA-2024-11 process, but the procedural-conflict mechanism in §4.3 continues to apply.

---

## Section 5 — Performance Metrics

5.1 **Tracked Metrics.**
   (a) Warranty claim turnaround (target: 30 days from claim to disposition);
   (b) OEM parts lead-time adherence;
   (c) Repeat-failure rate on OEM-replaced components within 24 months (a metric introduced in Addendum A).

5.2 **Note on Repeat Failures.** Operator's internal tracking shows multiple repeat forced-outage events on WIN-10131 and WIN-10011 following OEM-recommended interventions. See OIR-2025-007 and RCA-2025-004. The pattern is currently under Engineering review and is not yet the subject of formal warranty dispute.

---

## Section 6 — Limitations, Exclusions, Termination

6.1 **Exclusions.** Non-Siemens-Gamesa assets; consequential damages; lost generation revenue; environmental remediation.

6.2 **Non-OEM Parts Forfeiture.** Use of non-OEM parts on a covered unit voids Extended Service Contract coverage on the affected component.

6.3 **Termination.** OEM may terminate Extended Service Contract on 365 days' notice. Operator may terminate on 180 days' notice. Initial warranty period is non-terminable.

6.4 **Document Hierarchy (Within OEM Scope).** Where work is performed under this Agreement, VTM-SIEMENS-GAMESA-4-2 is the OEM's procedural authority. However, per Addendum A, **Operator Standard MS-2025-03 governs the decision to authorize replacement-class capital expenditure** even where the OEM's manual would mandate replacement at a lower threshold. The mechanism for routing such conflicts is MOC-2025-022.

---

## Addendum A — Acknowledged Procedural Conflict (Appended March 10, 2026)

**A.1 Gearbox Vibration Replacement Threshold.** Section 4 of VTM-SIEMENS-GAMESA-4-2 establishes a gearbox-replacement criterion at a measured vibration of **3.5 mm/s RMS** (running mean, broadband). Operator Standard MS-2025-03, §5.4 establishes the corresponding internal threshold at **5.0 mm/s RMS**, with refurbishment-first disposition between 5.0 and 7.0 mm/s RMS, and full replacement only at 7.0 mm/s RMS or above (or upon confirmed metallurgical evidence of imminent failure).

The conflict is acknowledged and is the subject of MOC-2025-022 ("Management of Change Request — Override Siemens Gamesa OEM Gearbox Vibration Replacement Threshold for Out-of-Warranty Units"), submitted March 2025 and currently in Engineering review as of the date of this Addendum.

**A.2 Practical Effect.** Until MOC-2025-022 is closed, OEM recommendations to replace gearboxes at measured vibration levels between 3.5 and 5.0 mm/s RMS will be flagged under §4.3 above and held for Operator Engineering disposition. Two such cases — WIN-10131 (October 2025) and WIN-10011 (December 2025) — are pending under RAM-2025-005 and RAM-2025-007 respectively.

**A.3 No Waiver.** Nothing in this Addendum waives any OEM warranty obligation or operator capital-replacement obligation. The Addendum addresses only the *procedural routing* of replacement-class recommendations.
