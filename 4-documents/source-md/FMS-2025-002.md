## Section 2 — Significant Events

**5/2 — WIN-10058 soc_drift outage, NW-PowerPool-WIND-014.**
GE turbine. State-of-charge drift on the auxiliary battery system tripped the unit at 14:22. Duration 227 hours. Lost generation 2,506.8 MWh. Full write-up in OIR-2025-006. Apex mobilized within four hours of CPS declining (they were two crews short, in May, in our region — this is what happens when you starve your scheduled vendor for four months). Apex invoice $621,000. Their recommendation was full battery-cabinet replacement. CPS, when I called them after the fact, said they would have refurbished it for under $150K. Same scope on paper, very different bill.

**5/19 — WIN-10011 vibration excess, NW-PowerPool-WIND-024.**
This is the unit whose January PM was on the deferred list (WO-2025-00119). Vibration excursion at 09:14. Took it offline at 10:01 controlled. Apex was on site by 18:00 the same day. Replaced two bearings. Invoice $389,000.

**5/26 — TRA-10561 vibration excess, NW-PowerPool-WIND-003.**
Siemens transformer at the WIND-003 substation. Vibration excess outage at 03:11 — a 158-hour outage that cost us 2,615.1 MWh of lost generation. See OIR-2025-007. Apex was on site by 11:00 with a Siemens Gamesa OEM subcontractor (the 1.8× rate uplift applies to that crew). They installed a vibration isolator and replaced two bushings. Invoice $446,000. RAM-2025-001 documents the repair-vs-replace decision we deferred at the time.

**Three Apex dispatches in one month.** Total $1,456,000. Average $485,000. The average has gone up since January.

- **Apex:** Three dispatches, $1,456,000. Their crew lead, Brad Holstein, is now on a first-name basis with my night dispatcher. That is not a relationship I wanted to develop.
- **CPS:** Limited to scheduled work outside the outage response window. Maria Tonkin sent me a polite-but-firm note on 5/15 asking when we would resume normal scheduling. I do not have an answer for her. The freeze formally lifted in March but the backlog is still consuming us.
- **Siemens Gamesa OEM service:** Two warranty inspections on WIN-10131 and WIN-10011, both with replacement recommendations totaling $1.2M per unit. Both declined pending MOC-2025-022.
- **Pinnacle:** One site visit for a previously scheduled scope on WIND-014. Their crew showed up with a service manual that referenced procedures from a 2019 predecessor-company manual. We had to stop work for half a day until the procedure conflict was resolved against our copy of the current Standard MS-2025-03.

---

## Section 4 — Open Issues / Backlog

- **Crew fatigue.** Three crew members have exceeded FMP-2's 60 hr/wk limit for two consecutive weeks. I filed a CAR (see CAR-2025-006 in process) on it because the policy says I have to. We have not had a safety event but the conditions are wrong.
- **Repeat-failure pattern.** WIN-10058 has now failed twice this calendar year (1/29 inspection-flagged trend, 5/2 outage). WIN-10011 has failed twice (5/19, prior March event). WIN-10131 has had a January overheating event and Siemens Gamesa wants $1.2M to replace it. These are not three separate stories; they are one story repeating at three turbines.
- **Vibration baseline drift on WIN-10016.** Inspections in late May show baseline shifted approximately 12% above March reading. Standard MS-2025-03 threshold is 15%. We are inside the band but the trend is wrong.
- **MOC-2025-022 still pending.** Two months in. Every Siemens Gamesa inspection produces a six-figure replacement recommendation we cannot evaluate against internal standard until this is resolved.

## Section 5 — Resource & Safety Notes

Crew of 11, plus two contractor technicians from CPS on rotation. Three FMP-2 exceedances logged (see CAR-2025-006). Two of those technicians had to take mandatory standdown weeks 5/27 and 6/3 — which dropped my available crew right when WIND-014 needed coverage.

Weather: a wet May. Eight days of restricted climbing. The 5/19 and 5/26 events both came after restricted-climbing days, which means inspection rounds we would otherwise have completed were pushed back.

One near-miss reported: 5/24, technician on WIN-10011 platform during pre-shutdown inspection lost footing on a wet grating. No injury. Logged.

## Section 6 — Forward Look

June will be worse before it is better. We have nineteen corrective WOs queued — most of which originated as preventive WOs we did not complete in Q1 or May. We need either (a) a CPS surge — and Maria has told me she can give me eight extra technicians if we commit through Q3 — or (b) continued Apex dispatches at $400K+ per event.

I am formally requesting through Karen that we authorize the CPS surge. It will cost us approximately $1.1M for the quarter. We have already spent $1.45M on Apex in May alone. I would rather have CPS on site fixing things before they fail than Apex on call fixing them after.

I will be filing CAR-2025-006 on crew fatigue this week.
