## 1. Outage Summary

TRA-10561 (NW-PowerPool-WIND-003 step-up transformer) tripped on tank-vibration relay action on 2025-05-25 at 21:07 PDT. Restoration completed 2025-06-01 at 11:07 PDT, total duration **158 hours**. Lost generation: **2,615.1 MWh**.

The event falls inside the Spring Vibration Cascade window (RCA-2025-001). Across May 2025 NW-PowerPool logged 19 forced outages, 11,360 MWh of lost generation. This event continues the pattern opened by OIR-2025-006 (WIN-10058) three weeks prior.

## 3. Telemetry Observations

- Tank vibration at trip: 4.1 mm/s (MS-2025-03 limit 3.5; Siemens Energy Manual SET-2200 limit 5.0).
- Top-oil at trip: 78.2 °C.
- DGA snapshot: acetylene 0.4 ppm (low), ethylene 22 ppm (elevated baseline 8), methane 11 ppm. Pattern consistent with mechanical loosening rather than electrical fault.
- Core-clamp torque on C-phase: measured 142 N·m (spec 240 N·m). Both clamps loosened.
- Quarterly core-clamp inspection (WO-2025-03059) was on the Q1 deferred list. Last documented inspection: 2024-09-12.
- Ambient: 18–22 °C, SW wind 4–8 m/s. Conditions normal.

Vibration baseline post-repair recorded 2.4 mm/s, slightly elevated from commissioning (2.0). The 12% baseline increase persisted to the December event (OIR-2025-004).

## 5. Preliminary Root Cause

Direct cause: progressive loosening of two C-phase core-clamp bolts driving sustained tank vibration above the MS-2025-03 alert threshold, ultimately tripping the vibration relay.

Contributing causes:
1. Quarterly core-clamp inspection deferred under the Q1 2025 budget freeze. The last documented torque check was September 2024 — 8 months prior to event.
2. Technician fatigue during the cascade. The lead technician (R. Vega) had logged 67.5 hours that week, exceeding FMP-2 limit of 60 hr/wk. This is documented in CAR-2025-001 (FMP-2 violations during Spring Vibration Cascade).
3. Apex's replacement recommendation appears to follow the pattern observed in other Period A events: emergency vendor consistently recommends replacement over field repair. Internal engineering successfully field-repaired. See EMR-2025-003 and RCA-2025-001.

## 6. Recommendations

1. Execute fleet-wide core-clamp torque audit on all NW-PowerPool transformers within 60 days. Track under CAR-2025-008.
2. Reinforce FMP-2 fatigue caps during emergency events. Refer to CAR-2025-001.
3. Add Buchholz residue trace evidence to standard post-outage telemetry capture. CSM-7 amendment to be drafted.
4. Reject Apex's transformer replacement quote ($1.86M). Internal field repair successful; replacement not warranted.
5. Cross-reference OIR-2025-004 (December 2025 overheating event on the same TRA-10561) and RCA-2025-001 (Spring Vibration Cascade root analysis).
