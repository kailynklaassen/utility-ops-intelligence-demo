## Section 2 — Significant Events

**11/12, 02:14 PT — SUB-10629 soc_drift outage, NW-PowerPool-WIND-024.**
Hitachi Energy substation equipment, battery management system. Same failure family as the WIN-10058 May event. State-of-charge drift through the night triggered a protective trip at 02:14. Duration 107 hours. Lost generation 2,609.0 MWh. OIR-2025-008 has the full write-up.

The BMS unit involved is the one whose firmware refresh has been on the deferred list since January (WO-2025-00153, see FMS-2025-001 for the original list). Eleven months deferred. Apex mobilized — fifth Apex deployment to WIND-024 in the trailing 12-month window. Their crew lead Brad Holstein arrived with the same playbook: replacement recommendation, $478,000 invoiced for the response, scope quoted at an additional $1.1M to replace the full BMS cabinet. Engineering is reviewing.

**11/17 — WIN-10016 GE turbine vibration follow-up, NW-PowerPool-WIND-014.**
Inspection-flagged vibration trend (recorded since May) crossed 14% above baseline. Standard MS-2025-03 threshold is 15%. We held against the threshold and proceeded with a planned bearing service. CPS on site, scheduled, $73,000. No emergency. Asset back in service in 4 days. This is what normal looks like, when we are allowed to do it.

**11/23 — WIN-10011 efficiency decline alert, NW-PowerPool-WIND-024.**
Pre-cursor signal for what would become OIR-2025-009 in early December. Did not trip. We logged the alert and queued an inspection.

**Three EPA activations in November.** All three at WIND-024. This is the fifth time in calendar 2025 that Apex has been on site at WIND-024.

- **Heartland Reliability Partners:** Site visit on 11/19 for the pilot scoping under VRA-2025-004. Walked WIND-024 with their senior engineer, Priya Subramanian. She made a comment that I want on the record because it is the most useful sentence anyone has spoken on this site in 18 months: "Your asset cohort is past the point where condition-based maintenance can save it. I told her that is what the next OER from Margaret was likely to say. She said: "Then you already know."

## Section 4 — Open Issues / Backlog

- **Five Apex deployments to WIND-024 in 12 months.** Brad Holstein's crew has been on site in January, May, June, August, and November. None of those responses resulted in a permanent fix. We are paying $400K–$700K per visit for symptomatic repair on assets that need replacement.
- **Deferred PM list now stands at 31 open WOs across the region.** That number is from Karen's tracker as of 11/28. Nine of them are older than 180 days.
- **MOC-2025-022 — eight months pending.**
- **CAR-2024-118 — one year and counting.**
- **CSM-7 cadence dispute — unresolved.**
- **WIN-10131** — quote in dispute, no determination from engineering.
- **TRA-10561** — Period A May event and pre-Period-B October vibration trend; the transformer is going to fail again before the December freeze if we don't act.

I will state the conclusion plainly for the record: **We are now in an emergency-repair cycle that we cannot break out of without a maintenance plan reset.** The current pattern is to fail, dispatch Apex, replace components, return to service, and fail again in 60–120 days. Five Apex deployments to WIND-024 in twelve months is not a maintenance program. It is a financial transfer from us to a vendor.
