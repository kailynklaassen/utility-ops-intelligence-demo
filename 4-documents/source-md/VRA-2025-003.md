# Vendor Repair Agreement VRA-2025-003

**Document ID:** VRA-2025-003
**Effective Date:** July 1, 2023
**Term:** Three years, expiring June 30, 2026 (renewal under review)
**Agreement Type:** Regional Service Agreement (Supplementary Scope)
**Parties:** Cascadia Renewable Operations LLC ("Operator") and Pinnacle Energy Maintenance, LLC ("Pinnacle" or "Contractor"), a Texas limited liability company.
**Governing Standard:** Cascadia Mechanical Standard MS-2025-03.
**Distribution:** Procurement; SPP Operations; MISO Operations; Compliance; Internal Audit; Pinnacle Account Manager.

---

## Preamble

This Regional Service Agreement establishes supplementary maintenance scope for Operator's generation and substation assets within the Southwest Power Pool (SPP) and the Midcontinent Independent System Operator (MISO) footprints, where the geographic reach of Cascadia Power Services Inc. (VRA-2025-001) is limited.

**Notice of Documentation Inconsistency.** Operator and Contractor acknowledge that a meaningful portion of Contractor's service procedures, work-instruction libraries, and inspection checklists were inherited from Contractor's predecessor entity, Pinnacle Industrial Services, Inc., which Contractor acquired in the second quarter of 2022. Several of those procedure documents continue to reference the predecessor entity's branding, document control numbers, and (in a small number of cases) deprecated work practices. The issue is the subject of an open corrective action — **CAR-2025-008** — and Contractor has committed to a full document-control reissue by no later than September 30, 2026. Until that reissue is complete, Operator's Regional Maintenance Planner is required, per §4.3 below, to cross-check Contractor's procedure citations against the current Cascadia Standard MS-2025-03 before approving Contractor work orders.

---

## Section 1 — Scope of Services

1.1 **Authorized Scope.** Contractor shall provide the following categories of work within SPP and MISO:

   (a) Scheduled preventive maintenance on wind generation assets, with specific competence in Nordex SE platforms (cross-reference: WIN-10007 at SPP-WIND-025);
   (b) Balance-of-plant electrical inspections;
   (c) Substation switchgear and protection-relay testing (cross-reference: VTM-ABB-SE7100 for ABB substation equipment);
   (d) Vibration and oil-analysis field services.

1.2 **Geographic Coverage.** SPP (primary) and MISO (secondary supplementary). NW-PowerPool and CAISO are out of scope.

1.3 **Documentation Discipline.** Contractor shall use only document-controlled procedures whose document numbers begin with the prefix "PEM-" (Pinnacle Energy Maintenance). Procedure documents bearing the legacy prefix "PIS-" (Pinnacle Industrial Services) are permitted only with explicit Operator approval and only until Contractor's document-control reissue is complete. See §4.3.

---

## Section 2 — Rate Schedule

2.1 **Hourly Labor Rate.** **$245.00 per technician-hour**, fully burdened. Four-hour minimum per dispatch.

2.2 **Premium Multipliers.**
   (a) Weekend / holiday: 1.35× standard rate;
   (b) Overnight: 1.20× standard rate;
   (c) Storm-emergency dispatch: 1.50× standard rate (only if EPA-2024-11 has not been activated for the same event).

2.3 **Parts Mark-Up.** 12% on documented parts invoice cost.

2.4 **Mobilization.** Travel within 350 miles of the Tulsa, OK hub included. Beyond 350 miles: $0.65/mile, half-rate travel time.

2.5 **Annual Escalation.** CPI-U (South Region), capped at 4.0%.

---

## Section 3 — Service Level Agreements

3.1 **Scheduled Work.** Contractor shall arrive on site within the mobilization window agreed at work-order issuance (typically 10–14 days).

3.2 **Storm Response.** Contractor shall mobilize within 12 hours of storm-driven dispatch where storm-emergency multiplier is invoked. This is **not** a substitute for emergency response under EPA-2024-11; see §3.3.

3.3 **EPA Boundary.** Contractor is not qualified for activation under EPA-2024-11. Emergency mobilization inside the 4-hour window is governed by VRA-2025-002 (Apex Emergency Grid Solutions).

---

## Section 4 — Approval & Authorization Workflow

4.1 **Work Authorization.** Work orders are issued by Operator's Regional Maintenance Planner. Cost approval thresholds match VRA-2025-001 (Regional Director above $75K; VP above $250K).

4.2 **Document Verification (Special Requirement).** Operator's Regional Maintenance Planner shall, prior to issuing any work order under this Agreement, verify that Contractor's referenced procedure citations:

   (a) Use the "PEM-" prefix (current Contractor document control), not the legacy "PIS-" prefix;
   (b) Are consistent with Cascadia Standard MS-2025-03;
   (c) Where in conflict with MS-2025-03, are flagged in the work order with a written deviation note and routed through Engineering for clearance.

4.3 **Open Corrective Action.** The pending corrective action on document-control inconsistency is **CAR-2025-008**, raised in March 2025. Closure is conditioned on Contractor's full reissue of its procedure library under "PEM-" document control. Status is reviewed at each quarterly business review.

4.4 **Out-of-Scope Recommendations.** Replacement-class recommendations from Contractor follow the same Repair Approval Memo workflow as Section 4.3 of VRA-2025-001.

---

## Section 5 — Performance Metrics

5.1 **Tracked Metrics.**
   (a) Procedure-citation compliance rate (target 100%; current observed: 89% per Q4 2025 audit);
   (b) Preventive maintenance schedule adherence;
   (c) MTTR for non-emergency corrective work;
   (d) Closure rate of "PIS-" legacy procedure references.

5.2 **Renewal Trigger.** Operator's renewal decision in mid-2026 is conditioned on full closure of CAR-2025-008 and a procedure-citation compliance rate of 98% or better in the preceding two quarters.

---

## Section 6 — Limitations, Exclusions, Termination

6.1 **Exclusions.**
   (a) Emergency mobilization inside the EPA-2024-11 window (see VRA-2025-002);
   (b) OEM-warranty work on Siemens Gamesa equipment (see VRA-2025-005);
   (c) Any procedure executed under a legacy "PIS-" document number unless the deviation note required by §4.2 has been completed.

6.2 **Termination for Cause.** Operator may terminate immediately for cause, including any field execution of a deprecated "PIS-" procedure in violation of §4.2.

6.3 **Termination for Convenience.** 120 days' written notice.

6.4 **Document Hierarchy.** In the event of conflict between this Agreement, Standard MS-2025-03, and Contractor's procedure library, Standard MS-2025-03 governs. Disputed Contractor procedure citations are referred to Engineering under MOC.
