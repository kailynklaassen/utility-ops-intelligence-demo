## Executive Summary

Through the first three quarters of FY2025, Cascadia Renewable Operations has paid **$5.8M** in emergency vendor invoices to Apex Emergency Grid Solutions ("Apex") against an equivalent scheduled-scope estimate of **$1.4M** from Cascadia Power Services Inc. ("CPS") under our 2023 multi-year MSA. The 4-to-1 cost differential is not a one-off anomaly. It is the direct result of repeated Emergency Procurement Authorization (EPA-2024-11) activations concentrated at a single region, NW-PowerPool, and a single site cluster within that region. All eleven emergency activations YTD have been at NW-PowerPool. Six of those eleven occurred during the May–June "Spring Vibration Cascade" (Period A) that is now fully closed in our accounting.

The financial picture is matched by a maintenance picture. Period A traces directly to fourteen preventive work orders that were deferred during the Q1 FY2025 budget freeze and never re-baselined. CAR-2025-001 (deferred PM cadence) and CAR-2025-004 (EPA activation pattern) document the upstream cause and the procurement consequence respectively. RCA-2025-001 ties the May 2 WIN-10058 outage and the May 26 TRA-10561 outage to the same deferred-PM root cause. We are paying premium emergency rates to repair failures that scheduled preventive work would have prevented at a fraction of the cost.

I am requesting executive action on three items before year-end close: (1) renegotiation of the Apex master rate card and EPA threshold; (2) acceleration of the Heartland Reliability Partners pilot under VRA-2025-004 to introduce competitive emergency capacity; and (3) a formal compliance review of EPA-2024-11 itself. The current procurement framework financially rewards reactive replacement over preventive repair.

## Section 3 — Root Causes

Three documented root causes converge:

**3.1 Deferred preventive maintenance — CAR-2025-001.**
Fourteen preventive WOs at NW-PowerPool-WIND-014 and -024 were deferred during the Q1 2025 budget freeze. Twelve of those fourteen were on assets that subsequently triggered Period A outages. RCA-2025-001 establishes the causal chain for WIN-10058 and TRA-10561 specifically.

**3.2 EPA-2024-11 threshold abuse — CAR-2025-004.**
EPA-2024-11 allows competitive-bid bypass when mobilization is required within four hours. The four-hour threshold has been invoked in cases where the underlying failure mode was known days or weeks in advance (e.g., the August re-dispatch to WIN-10131 followed an internal vibration alarm logged 11 days prior). CAR-2025-004 documents seven of the eleven activations where a non-emergency procurement path was available but not taken.

**3.3 OEM replacement-vs-repair conflict — MOC-2025-022.**
Apex subcontracts a portion of its turbine work to Siemens Gamesa OEM technicians at a 1.8× rate. Siemens Gamesa OEM Manual Rev. 4.2 mandates full component replacement at vibration thresholds 30% below the revised internal Standard MS-2025-03. MOC-2025-022, the management-of-change request to formally override the OEM threshold, has been pending engineering review since March 2025. In its absence, field crews default to OEM guidance — and replacement scope.

- **Cascadia Power Services MSA** — at risk of being characterized as "non-responsive" because every NW-PowerPool dispatch has gone to Apex. CPS performance metrics will look weak through no fault of CPS.

## Section 5 — Recommended Executive Action

1. **Issue notice of renegotiation to Apex Emergency Grid Solutions.** Target a 25% reduction on labor rate, removal of the 35% emergency parts mark-up in favor of cost-plus-15%, and a cap on weekend multiplier exposure. Estimated FY26 savings: $1.8–2.4M at current dispatch volume.
2. **Accelerate Heartland Reliability Partners pilot (VRA-2025-004).** Heartland's $215/hr labor rate and performance-based contract structure introduce competitive tension. Approve a six-month pilot in NW-PowerPool with three named activation tests, starting Q4 FY2025.
3. **Open a formal compliance review of EPA-2024-11.** Recommend revising the four-hour mobilization threshold to require documented prior-knowledge attestation, blocking EPA activation when the failure mode was known and a CPS scheduled path was viable.
4. **Resolve MOC-2025-022.** Engineering review has been pending nine months. Assign an owner with a 30-day deadline.

## Section 6 — Decision Required

I request executive sign-off on items 1–3 above by **2025-10-15**, to allow procurement to issue the Apex notice and Heartland pilot terms ahead of Period B winter operating conditions. Item 4 requires only an owner assignment and can be resolved at the next operations review.
