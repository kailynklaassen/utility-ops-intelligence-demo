## 1. Outage Summary

WIN-10131 tripped on gearbox over-temperature on 2025-08-27 at 20:43 PDT, returning to service on 2025-09-04 at 18:43 PDT after a duration of **190 hours**. Total lost generation: **4,632.9 MWh**. This is the second multi-day overheating event on WIN-10131 in FY2025; the first was the January event recorded in OIR-2025-005 (139 h, 3,389.3 MWh).

This unit has now experienced 8 forced outages in FY2025 totaling 17,239 MWh of lost generation. The cumulative pattern is the subject of MOC-2025-022 (Siemens Gamesa OEM threshold override) and RCA-2025-005 (procedural shortcut analysis).

## 3. Telemetry Observations

- Gearbox sump oil temperature at trip: 91.4 °C (limit 90).
- Main bearing temperature at trip: 96.2 °C.
- Vibration RMS on main shaft: 7.8 mm/s (Cascadia MS-2025-03 limit 6.5; OEM 10.0).
- Iron particulate in oil: 18 ppm.
- The CSM-7 quarterly oil-spectrometer test scheduled for July 2025 (WO-2025-07212) was closed as "deferred to next scheduled outage" per technician notes — see CAR-2025-006 (CSM-7 procedural shortcut).
- Ambient conditions: 28–31 °C daytime, 4.2 m/s SW wind. No icing or storm-related stressor.

CPS scheduled equivalent estimate (issued 2025-09-10 for retrospective benchmark): $96,500. See EMR-2025-002 for invoice detail and VRA-2025-003 for the Apex MSA pricing schedule.

Siemens Gamesa OEM has issued quotation Q-SG-25-0844 for full gearbox replacement at **$1.2M**. The recommendation is based on vibration RMS exceeding OEM Manual Rev. 4.2 threshold of 6.0 mm/s on the high-speed shaft (measured 6.4 mm/s on the second restart attempt). Cascadia internal engineering disputes the replacement recommendation under MOC-2025-022; refer to RAM-2025-004.

## 5. Preliminary Root Cause

Direct cause: degraded gearbox oil with elevated iron particulate, leading to inadequate heat transfer in the sump heat exchanger and runaway bearing temperature under summer ambient load.

Contributing causes:
1. Deferral of CSM-7 oil-spectrometer test (WO-2025-07212) — refer to CAR-2025-006.
2. Continued operation between 82 °C and 90 °C bearing temperature, permitted by OEM manual but flagged by Standard MS-2025-03; pending MOC-2025-022.
3. WIN-10131 had a prior overheating event in January 2025 (OIR-2025-005) without root-cause closure — the deferred Q1 work was not re-prioritized following the January event.

## 6. Recommendations

1. Close MOC-2025-022 with a documented Cascadia-specific operating threshold. Pending engineering review since March 2025 (9 months as of this report).
2. Reinstate the CSM-7 quarterly oil-spectrometer step at NW-PowerPool. Track under CAR-2025-006.
3. Reject the Siemens Gamesa $1.2M gearbox replacement quote pending Cascadia engineering evaluation. Refer to RAM-2025-004 and RCA-2025-003.
4. Add WIN-10131 to the FY26 capital planning watchlist alongside WIN-10011 and WIN-10130 (RCA-2025-003).
5. Cross-reference RCA-2025-002 (Fall Overheating Sequence) for the broader pattern.
