## Executive Summary

WIN-10130 at CAISO-WIND-026 logged **14 forced outages** and **20,494 MWh** of lost generation in FY2025 — the worst single-asset outcome in the Cascadia fleet. The event detail is captured in OIR-2025-010.

The root cause is **a policy-conflict deadlock between Siemens Gamesa OEM Manual Rev. 4.2 and Cascadia internal Standard MS-2025-03**, with **MOC-2025-022 the unresolved instrument intended to break the deadlock**. The deadlock has now persisted for over 10 months. During that time WIN-10130 has been repair-cycled 14 times, costing Cascadia approximately $2.6M in vendor invoices and producing the largest revenue loss on any single asset in FY2025.

The WIN-10130 pattern is not isolated. Three NW-PowerPool sister-units (WIN-10131, WIN-10011, WIN-10054) show similar trend slopes and are governed by the same policy conflict. A coordinated FY26 capital plan is the appropriate response.

## 1. Failure Mode Classification

**M-3 (Mechanical Cumulative):** Across FY2025, WIN-10130 displayed continuous degradation rather than discrete failures. Iron particulate in gearbox oil rose from 6 ppm (Jan) to 14 ppm (Oct). Vibration RMS rose from 5.2 mm/s (Jan baseline) to 7.4 mm/s (Oct peak). Heat-exchanger fouling increased over the year. Each repair cycle has restored the unit to within a narrowing window of pre-failure state.

**V-1 (Vendor Governance):** Three different field service contractors handled the 14 events (CPS, Apex, Heartland). Service history is fragmented; no single vendor has a coherent view of the asset's condition. Repair recommendations have been inconsistent. Re-baselining occurs at each vendor handoff.

**P-2 (Policy Conflict):** Siemens Gamesa OEM Manual Rev. 4.2 specifies replacement at vibration RMS 6.0 mm/s on the high-speed shaft. Cascadia Standard MS-2025-03 sets the threshold at 8.0 mm/s. WIN-10130 operated above the OEM threshold for **178 cumulative hours** in FY2025 without replacement. The OEM has issued a $1.2M gearbox replacement quote (Q-SG-25-0211, issued 2025-04-30). The quote has been rejected pending MOC-2025-022 resolution; MOC-2025-022 has been pending engineering review since 2025-03-04.

## 2. Fishbone / Contributing Factors

**Method:**
- MOC-2025-022 is the unresolved instrument. It was opened 2025-03-04. As of report date (2026-02-04), 11 months elapsed. The MOC has been routed three times for engineering review; each routing has stalled. The proximate cause of each stall is unclear and is a governance concern.
- Standard MS-2025-03 was issued February 2025 with thresholds 30% above the prior internal standard (which matched the OEM). The decision to raise the thresholds was supported by an internal study referenced in MS-2025-03 §2 but the study is not attached to the MOC.

**Machine:**
- WIN-10130 has been in service 4.3 years (commission date 2021-09). 12 sister-units in the same product line are deployed across CAISO-WIND-026 (4 units) and NW-PowerPool-WIND-024 (8 units). Mean service age across the population: 3.8 years.
- The product line was the first Siemens Gamesa generation to ship with the upgraded high-speed-shaft bearing assembly. OEM monitoring data (shared under VRA-2025-001) indicates that the upgraded bearing requires the lower vibration threshold cited in Rev. 4.2.

**Money:**
- Apex invoiced $1.91M against this asset in FY2025 (6 events). Average per-event $318K. Three Apex recommendations to replace components were rejected; the field repairs that followed were ~25% of quoted cost.
- The $1.2M gearbox replacement quote has been outstanding since April 2025. Had it been executed in May 2025, the avoided revenue-loss in H2 FY25 would have been approximately $740K (Aug–Dec events on this asset, 6 events × ~$120K each).

**Materials:**
- The Cascadia OEM service-bulletin intake captured Siemens Gamesa bulletin SG-PB-2025-02 (April 2025) recommending pitch accumulator pre-charge verification every 6 months. The bulletin is implicated in the WIN-10011 December event (OIR-2025-009) for the sister-fleet at NW-PowerPool-WIND-024.

**The economic decision is unambiguous.** Replacing the gearbox would have cost less than continued repair-cycling, even before counting lost generation revenue. The deadlock between OEM Manual Rev. 4.2 and Standard MS-2025-03 is materially costing Cascadia roughly $2.65M per affected asset per year.

For the 12-unit sister-fleet, applying the same arithmetic: gross potential impact ~$31.8M annually. Even discounting heavily for asset variability, the FY26 capital case is compelling.

## 5. Long-Term Corrective Recommendations

1. **Force resolution of MOC-2025-022 within 30 days.** Two outcomes are acceptable:
   - (a) Adopt OEM Manual Rev. 4.2 threshold of 6.0 mm/s. Budget $4.8M for gearbox replacement on the 4 most-affected sister-units (WIN-10130, -10131, -10011, -10054) in FY26 Q1.
   - (b) Formally adopt MS-2025-03 threshold of 8.0 mm/s as controlling, void the Siemens Gamesa replacement quote, and update the MSA to disable OEM replacement-quote workflow.
   The current limbo is the worst outcome.
2. **Single-prime vendor at CAISO-WIND-026** for the next 12 months. Recommend either CPS or Heartland (not Apex). Re-establish coherent service history. Refer to VRA-2025-006.
3. **FY26 capital planning workstream** for the 12-unit sister-fleet across CAISO-WIND-026 and NW-PowerPool-WIND-024. Cross-reference RAM-2025-007.
4. **OEM engagement formalization.** Open a quarterly OEM technical review with Siemens Gamesa to surface their monitoring data and reconcile with internal vibration baselines. Refer to VRA-2025-001.
5. Add WIN-10130, -10131, -10011, -10054 to a single asset-watch list governed by this RCA's corrective plan.

## 6. Vendor Performance Commentary

**Siemens Gamesa OEM** has applied Manual Rev. 4.2 consistently. The OEM is not behaving unreasonably; they are applying their published manual. The conflict is internal to Cascadia.

**Apex Emergency Grid Solutions** has invoiced $1.91M against this asset in FY2025 (~73% of total vendor cost on WIN-10130). Apex's repeated replacement recommendations on this asset's components have been the primary driver of internal engineering counter-proposals. The pattern matches the Period A and Period B observations in RCA-2025-001 and RCA-2025-002.

**Heartland Reliability Partners** has handled three events on this asset at lower cost than Apex with comparable outcome quality. Heartland's performance-based contract proposal (referenced in narrative bible §2.7) should be re-examined for CAISO operations.

**Cascadia Power Services Inc.** has handled four events on this asset at lowest unit cost. CPS lacks the depot footprint to be a single-prime at CAISO; investment in a Bakersfield expansion would be required if single-prime CPS is selected.
