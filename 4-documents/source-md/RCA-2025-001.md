## Executive Summary

Across May and June 2025, NW-PowerPool logged **40 forced outages** and **24,586 MWh** of lost generation across two sites (WIND-014 and WIND-024). The cluster spans GE turbines (WIN-10058, WIN-10016), Siemens Gamesa turbines (WIN-10011, WIN-10131), the ABB substation interconnect (SUB-10641), and the Hitachi Energy substation (SUB-10629), plus a Siemens Energy transformer (TRA-10561) at the neighboring WIND-003 site. The cluster is not coincidental.

The proximate trigger was a series of mechanical and SoC-drift faults. The root cause is **systemic deferral of preventive maintenance** at NW-PowerPool-WIND-014 and -024 during the Q1 2025 budget freeze, compounded by FMP-2 fatigue-policy violations as the cascade unfolded, and amplified by emergency-vendor cost behavior. Period A emergency-vendor invoicing totaled **$4.6M** with Apex Emergency Grid Solutions, against an estimated CPS equivalent of $1.16M (4× ratio).

## 2. Fishbone / Contributing Factors

**Manpower:**
- FMP-2 (60 hr/wk fatigue cap) exceeded by 4 of 7 lead technicians during the cascade. R. Vega logged 67.5 hours in week of 2025-05-19; M. Sandoval 64.0 hours in week of 2025-05-26. CAR-2025-001 tracks the FMP-2 violations and recommends technician staffing reform.
- Pinnacle Energy Maintenance contracted for surge support; their close-out documentation does not consistently capture sub-step verification (e.g., pre-charge checks). See CAR-2025-004.

**Method:**
- Standard MS-2025-03 sets 6,000-hour bearing reinspection on GE units. GE OEM Manual GRE-2200 lists 8,000 hours, predating the 2023 firmware refresh. CAR-2025-003 has been open since the firmware refresh; the OEM-vs-Cascadia conflict has not been formally resolved.
- ABB Substation Manual SE-7100 §6.4 specifies a 4-year substation maintenance cycle. MS-2025-03 reduced this to 2 years. Documentation conflict noted; refer to RCA-2025-004.

**Machine:**
- WIN-10058 main bearing was at 7,420 operating hours since last inspection — 1,420 hours past MS-2025-03.
- TRA-10561 last documented core-clamp torque check: 2024-09-12 (8 months prior to event).
- SUB-10641 last major outage was 2024-10-15; major-cycle inspection was deferred under Q1 freeze.

**Materials:**
- Hitachi Energy BMS firmware 2.7.1 on multiple battery buffers — defect documented in SB-HE-2024-09. Fleet-wide upgrade plan tracked under CAR-2024-118.

**Measurement:**
- SoC drift > 2.0% is classified "advisory" under current CSM-7 §4.2 but the actual protection action occurs at the relay co-ordination level (typically 2.5–3.5%). Misalignment between operator awareness and relay action is implicated in 3 of the cascade events.

**Money:**
- The Q1 2025 budget freeze deferred 14 preventive WOs at NW-PowerPool. The deferred list includes WO-2025-01044 (WIN-10131 PM, later linked to OIR-2025-005), WO-2025-04022 (WIN-10058 bearing, OIR-2025-006), WO-2025-03059 (TRA-10561 core-clamp, OIR-2025-007), and 11 others. **This is the principal root cause.**

## 5. Long-Term Corrective Recommendations

1. **Close CAR-2024-118 with funded action plan.** Cadence reform for aging wind assets has been pending for 7 months. Recommended: 6,000-hour bearing inspection cycle, 2-year substation cycle, quarterly oil-spectrometer, semi-annual core-clamp torque. Funding estimate $2.4M FY26.
2. **Close MOC-2025-022.** The OEM-vs-Cascadia threshold conflict is the dominant single-asset driver of WIN-10131 / WIN-10011 events. Either accept the Siemens Gamesa replacement quote ($1.2M per unit × 4 units = $4.8M) or formally adopt MS-2025-03 as controlling and discontinue OEM quote review. Status: pending engineering review since 2025-03-04.
3. **Address vendor cost arbitrage.** Cascadia is paying Apex 4× CPS rates for equivalent scope. The pricing differential is structural to the Apex MSA (Schedule B-3, 1.5× weekend multiplier, 35% emergency parts mark-up, 1.8× OEM subcontract multiplier). VRA renegotiation should be initiated; refer to VRA-2025-003.
4. **Reform technician fatigue management.** FMP-2 cap was exceeded by 4 lead techs during the cascade. Surge staffing must not be loaded onto the lead pool. See CAR-2025-001.
5. **Audit Pinnacle Energy Maintenance close-out quality.** See CAR-2025-004.

## 6. Vendor Performance Commentary

**Apex Emergency Grid Solutions** invoiced $4.6M across Period A. Per-event average $385K. Consistently recommends component replacement; recommendation pattern coincides with their parts mark-up (35%). Three replacement recommendations during the period (TRA-10561 transformer replacement, WIN-10058 BMS module set, SUB-10641 relay panel) were rejected by internal engineering and successfully field-repaired at ~25% of quoted cost.

**Cascadia Power Services Inc.** failed to meet 4-hour mobilization SLA on 6 occasions during the cascade. Root cause: concurrent dispatch from a single Bend, OR depot. Recommend a second NW-PowerPool depot (Pendleton or The Dalles) to maintain SLA.

**Siemens Gamesa OEM** (operating through Apex 1.8× subcontract) issued replacement quotes that align to OEM Manual Rev. 4.2 thresholds. Quotes are not unreasonable under the OEM manual but conflict with MS-2025-03. The MOC instrument is the correct path; engineering review must be unblocked.

**GE Renewable Energy** OEM Manual GRE-2200 inspection intervals continue to lag the 2023 firmware refresh. CAR-2025-003 is in OEM-engagement phase.
