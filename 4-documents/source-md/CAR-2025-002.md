## 1. Finding

Siemens Gamesa Renewable Energy OEM Service Manual **Rev. 4.2** (document reference **VTM-SIEMENS-GAMESA-4.2**), §3.2.1 "Drivetrain Vibration Limits," prescribes mandatory component replacement (gearbox + high-speed-shaft bearing assembly) when measured nacelle-floor RMS velocity exceeds **4.5 mm/s** averaged across a 10-minute window at rated power, per ISO 10816-21:2015 Class IV. The internally revised **Cascadia Standard MS-2025-03 §5.2**, issued February 2025, raises the action threshold to **6.5 mm/s** with a mandatory refurbishment-first protocol (bearing replacement and oil flush) before any gearbox replacement decision.

This is a **30% relative threshold gap** and a different action class (replace-on-trip vs. refurbish-first). The result is that field engineering teams, when invoking Siemens Gamesa under OEM warranty, are receiving replacement recommendations that Cascadia Engineering would not authorize under MS-2025-03.

Management of Change request **MOC-2025-022** was submitted on 2025-03-18 to formally override the OEM threshold for non-warranty operating decisions. The MOC has been in "Pending Engineering Review" status for 14 months as of issue date of this CAR.

## 2. Evidence

|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| WIN-10130 | OUT-2025-0079 | 2025-02-11 | 5.1 mm/s | Replace gearbox | Refurbish bearing | Replaced (OEM warranty pressure) — $1.2M |
| WIN-10131 | OUT-2025-0570 | 2025-08-28 | 5.8 mm/s | Replace gearbox | Refurbish bearing | Disputed; replacement not executed pending MOC outcome |
| WIN-10011 | OUT-2025-0320 | 2025-07-08 | 5.4 mm/s | Replace gearbox | Refurbish bearing | Refurbished by CPS — $214K |

Two replacements totalling **$2.4M** were executed in FY2025 on the basis of the OEM threshold; under MS-2025-03 these would have remained in the refurbish-first protocol at ~$214K each, for an avoidable cost differential of approximately **$1.97M**.

### 2.2 Warranty interaction

Siemens Gamesa has stated, in correspondence dated 2025-04-09 (filed VTM-SIEMENS-GAMESA-4.2 supplemental), that "operation above the Rev. 4.2 §3.2.1 limits voids the drivetrain extended warranty for the asset for the remainder of the warranty period." Warranty Administration has interpreted this as binding. Engineering disagrees.

### 2.3 Repeat-failure correlation

WIN-10131 has 8 forced outages YTD; WIN-10011 has 12. The OEM "replace at trip" rule has not produced a step-change reduction in outage rate on assets where it has been executed (WIN-10130 has had 14 forced outages in FY2025, including post-gearbox-replacement events — see OIR-2025-006).

## 3. Root Cause Hypothesis

- The Siemens Gamesa Rev. 4.2 thresholds are calibrated to ISO 10816-21:2015 Class IV "alarm" levels, which are designed for protection of individual asset OEM components rather than fleet-level reliability optimization. Application of Class IV alarm-as-action policy is conservative from the OEM's warranty perspective but financially punitive for the operator.
- Cascadia Standard MS-2025-03 was revised in February 2025 to align with IEC 61400-25-6 condition-monitoring guidance and internal vibration-trending data from MISO-WIND-038 (the Vestas baseline fleet — see VTM-VESTAS-2025 §7), which shows successful refurbishment outcomes at 6.0–6.8 mm/s RMS.
- MOC-2025-022 has stalled because closure requires concurrence from Warranty Administration, which has not been able to obtain written acknowledgment from Siemens Gamesa that an override would not void warranty.

## 4. Conflict With Existing Guidance

This finding is, at its core, a documented conflict between:

1. **VTM-SIEMENS-GAMESA-4.2 §3.2.1** (OEM-authored, warranty-binding).
2. **Cascadia Standard MS-2025-03 §5.2** (internal, refurbishment-first).
3. **MOC-2025-022** (pending; would formalize override of #1 in favor of #2).

The conflict is not theoretical. It has produced two replacement decisions on WIN-10130 and WIN-10054 that Engineering would have classified as refurbish-eligible. It is also the procedural reason that the WIN-10131 gearbox replacement quote (filed under EMR-2025-005) remains contested.

## 5. Recommended Corrective Action

1. Escalate MOC-2025-022 to Engineering Standards Committee for a forced decision within 60 days. The 14-month pendency is itself a control failure.
2. Issue an interim **Engineering Concession Letter (ECL-2025-009)** authorizing the MS-2025-03 threshold for all non-warranty-period assets effective immediately, while MOC-2025-022 closure is pursued.
3. Direct Warranty Administration to obtain a formal written position from Siemens Gamesa on the warranty implications of operation between 4.5 and 6.5 mm/s RMS. If no response is received within 90 days, the absence of objection shall be treated as constructive acceptance.
4. Until MOC closure, all OEM replacement recommendations triggered solely by the §3.2.1 threshold (i.e., measured velocity 4.5–6.5 mm/s) require Engineering co-sign before procurement. No exceptions delegated to site.
5. Retroactively review the WIN-10130 (OUT-2025-0079) and WIN-10054 (OUT-2025-0114) replacement decisions for cost-recovery applicability.
