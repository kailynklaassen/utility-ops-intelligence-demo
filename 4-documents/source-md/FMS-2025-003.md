## Section 2 — Significant Events

**10/7, 04:47 PT — WIN-10085 overheating, NW-PowerPool-WIND-003.**
Vestas turbine. Gearbox lube circuit temperature climbed steadily from 02:30 and tripped at 04:47. Unit auto-shut. Duration 226 hours. Lost generation 3,740.6 MWh — our largest single-asset event of the fall.

The painful part: the summer service window on this unit had a cooling-system maintenance WO (WO-2025-01142) on the schedule for July 23. We deferred it because the access road through the Mount Hood National Forest was closed for wildfire response from July 14 through August 22. By the time access reopened, the WIN-10085 service had been pushed to "next service window" because the crew was reassigned to outage response at WIND-024.

EMR-2025-004 documents the field response. The CPS recommendation in that EMR is worth quoting verbatim because it stands in contrast to what we usually get from Apex:

> *"Recommend full preventive cooling-loop service (oil exchange, spectrometer sample, filter replacement, hose-set inspection, coolant chemistry restoration). Estimated parts and labor: $185,000. Bearing condition reviewed — within tolerance per Standard MS-2025-03; no replacement indicated. Estimated unit return to service: 8 days."*

CPS quoted $185K for a refurbishment that returned WIN-10085 to operating condition. Compare to the Apex pattern at WIND-024 across May–August, where every dispatch produced a replacement recommendation between $400K and $1.2M. Same kind of asset, same kind of failure mode, very different vendor philosophy.

**10/22 — SUB-10641 substation breaker re-trip, NW-PowerPool-WIND-014.**
Second event on this breaker this year. CPS on site within 8 hours of declaration; not an EPA event. Refurbished the operating mechanism and recalibrated the trip unit. Down 18 hours, 84 MWh lost. Cost $46,000 against an Apex quote estimate of $185–220K had we activated EPA. I am noting this because it is the cleanest example of the cost differential we have on the books for this period.

**10/29 — WIN-10131 vibration excursion, NW-PowerPool-WIND-024.**
Returned to controlled state without an outage. Crew flagged a follow-up requirement. This is the same unit that has been on the Siemens Gamesa $1.2M replacement quote since June.

## Section 3 — Vendor Activity

- **CPS:** Eight scheduled WOs completed, one emergency-equivalent response on WIN-10085 (10/7) and SUB-10641 (10/22). Maria Tonkin's surge crew is the reason October read this well. Total CPS invoiced: $387,000 across both response and scheduled work.
- **Apex:** Two dispatches earlier in the period for trailing follow-up on WIN-10011 (Period A residual) and a precautionary visit to WIN-10131 on 10/14. Combined $689,000.
- **Siemens Gamesa OEM service:** No activity in October (their next scheduled visit is November 4). The $1.2M WIN-10131 quote remains in dispute pending MOC-2025-022.

This is consistent with our experience that Vestas, as an OEM, behaves differently from Siemens Gamesa on the replace-vs-repair question.

## Section 4 — Open Issues / Backlog

- **Cooling-system maintenance deferrals from summer.** Four WIND-024 turbines and two WIND-003 turbines had cooling-system PMs pushed from July to "next available window" because of wildfire access restrictions. Three of those remain open. EMR-2025-004 has been folded into the CSM-7 procedural review that engineering is supposedly running.
- **CSM-7 oil-spectrometer test.** The procedure is still ambiguous on whether the test is quarterly (CPS interpretation) or annual (Apex interpretation). For WIN-10085 specifically, the last oil-spectrometer sample is dated April 2025 — six months pre-event. Quarterly cadence would have caught it.
- **Crew fatigue carry-over from Period A.** CAR-2025-006 is still open. We have not had an exceedance in October but two technicians remain on reduced rotation per FMP-2.
- **MOC-2025-022 — still pending.** Seven months.
